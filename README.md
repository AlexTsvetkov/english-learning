# English Improvement Course: Psychology & Personal Growth

## 12-Week B2→C1 Course for Russian Speakers

A comprehensive English course combining **grammar**, **vocabulary**, **reading**, and **essay writing** — all centered around psychology, mental health, emotions, and personal growth.

---

## Course Structure

Each week contains 4 modules:

| Module | Folder | Time | Description |
|--------|--------|------|-------------|
| 📘 Grammar | `grammar/` | ~45 min | C1-level grammar with exercises and contextual writing |
| 📗 Vocabulary | `vocabulary/` | ~30 min | 20–25 target words, phrasal verbs, idioms, and practice |
| 📖 Reading | `reading/` | ~20 min | Engaging article using the week's vocabulary, with glossary and discussion questions |
| 📙 Writing | `essay/` | ~45 min | Model text, writing technique, and a guided essay task |

---

## How to Practice This Course

### Step-by-Step Weekly Workflow

Follow this order each week for the best results:

1. **Start with Grammar** (Monday, ~45 min)
   - Read the explanation carefully — compare with Russian where noted
   - Do all exercises (fill-in-the-gap, error correction, sentence transformation)
   - Complete the contextual writing task at the end

2. **Learn Vocabulary** (Tuesday, ~30 min)
   - Study the 20–25 target words: read definitions, examples, and Russian translations
   - Pay attention to collocations and register notes
   - Do all practice exercises (matching, fill-in-the-blank, odd-one-out, translation)
   - Write your own sentences using 5 target words

3. **Read the Article** (Wednesday, ~20 min)
   - Read the article in `reading/` — it uses vocabulary from the same week
   - Don't look up every word; try to understand from context first
   - Check the glossary at the end for key terms
   - Answer the discussion questions (aloud or in writing)

4. **Write the Essay** (Thursday, ~45 min)
   - Study the model text and its analysis
   - Learn the week's writing technique
   - Plan and write your essay (250–350 words) using the provided template
   - Read your essay aloud to catch mistakes

5. **Review & Reflect** (Friday–Weekend, ~15–20 min)
   - Review vocabulary — re-read word lists, test yourself
   - Re-read your essay and the model text
   - Write a short self-reflection journal entry (100–150 words, freewriting)
   - Review grammar notes and redo any exercises you found difficult

### Weekly Schedule (Recommended)

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of This Course

1. **Go week by week** — don't skip ahead; each week builds on the previous one
2. **Keep a vocabulary notebook** — write new words with collocations and example sentences
3. **Actually write the essays** — this is where the biggest growth happens
4. **Read your writing aloud** — catches mistakes and builds fluency
5. **Use the reading articles actively** — underline new words, try to retell the article
6. **Answer discussion questions** — practice speaking by answering out loud (even to yourself)
7. **Use the self-reflection journal** — even 5 minutes of free writing in English helps
8. **Review weekly** — every Sunday, spend 15 minutes reviewing the past week
9. **Track difficult words** — keep a personal list of words you keep forgetting and review them daily
10. **Don't fear mistakes** — the exercises are for learning, not for testing

## Using AI as a Tutor

This course was designed to work well with AI assistants. You can:

- **Check your essay**: Paste your essay into ChatGPT/Claude and ask: *"Check this essay for grammar, vocabulary, and coherence. I'm a B2→C1 Russian speaker."*
- **Practice speaking**: Read discussion questions aloud and answer them, or use an AI voice assistant
- **Get exercise feedback**: Paste your exercise answers and ask the AI to check them
- **Ask for explanations**: If a grammar rule is unclear, ask the AI to explain it with more examples
- **Generate more practice**: Ask the AI to create additional exercises on any topic you find difficult

The file `COURSE_PROMPT.md` contains the full prompt used to generate this course — you can use it to generate additional materials or customize the course.
