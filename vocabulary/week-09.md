## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **live up to** | to meet expectations | соответствовать (ожиданиям) | She felt constant pressure to live up to her parents' expectations. |
| **measure up (to)** | to be good enough compared to a standard | дотягивать (до стандарта) | He never felt like he could measure up to his older brother. |
| **fit in** | to belong socially | вписаться, быть своим | She spent years trying to fit in before realizing she was enough as she was. |
| **stand out** | to be noticeably different | выделяться | It takes courage to stand out instead of conforming. |
| **come across as** | to give an impression; to seem | производить впечатление | He comes across as confident, but inside he's deeply insecure. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **true to yourself** | acting in accordance with your real nature | верный себе | Never have I felt so free as when I started being true to myself. |
| **a wolf in sheep's clothing** | someone who hides their true nature | волк в овечьей шкуре | His kindness was a facade — he was a wolf in sheep's clothing. |
| **find your footing** | to become comfortable in a new situation | обрести почву под ногами | After years of pretending, she finally found her footing. |
| **own it** | to confidently accept who you are | принять и не стесняться | You're different from others — own it. |
