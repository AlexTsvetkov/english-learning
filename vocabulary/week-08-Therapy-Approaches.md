## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: General Therapy Terms

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **therapist** (n) | a trained professional who provides therapy | терапевт, психотерапевт | Finding the right therapist can take time. | see a therapist, licensed therapist | neutral |
| **therapeutic** (adj) | having a healing effect | терапевтический | Writing can be a deeply therapeutic process. | therapeutic relationship/approach/effect | neutral |
| **session** (n) | a single meeting with a therapist | сессия, сеанс | She felt drained after an intense therapy session. | therapy session, weekly session | neutral |
| **intervention** (n) | a specific technique or action taken to help | интервенция, вмешательство | Early intervention can prevent long-term damage. | therapeutic/early intervention, psychological intervention | formal |
| **breakthrough** (n) | a sudden, significant moment of progress | прорыв | She had a breakthrough when she connected her anger to childhood neglect. | make/have a breakthrough, major breakthrough | neutral |
| **rapport** (n) | trust and understanding between therapist and client | раппорт | Building rapport is essential before deeper work can begin. | build/establish rapport, therapeutic rapport | neutral |
| **confidentiality** (n) | keeping information private and secret | конфиденциальность | Confidentiality is the cornerstone of the therapeutic relationship. | maintain/ensure confidentiality, breach of confidentiality | formal |

### Category 2: CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **CBT** | therapy focused on changing unhelpful thought and behavior patterns | КПТ (когнитивно-поведенческая терапия) | CBT is one of the most evidence-based forms of therapy. | CBT techniques, undergo CBT | neutral |
| **automatic thought** | an involuntary thought that arises in response to a situation | автоматическая мысль | CBT helps you identify and challenge automatic thoughts. | negative automatic thought, identify automatic thoughts | formal |
| **thought record** | a written log of thoughts, emotions, and evidence | дневник мыслей | Keeping a thought record helped her see patterns in her thinking. | complete/keep a thought record | formal |
| **behavioral experiment** | testing a belief by trying something new | поведенческий эксперимент | The therapist suggested a behavioral experiment to test her fear. | conduct/design a behavioral experiment | formal |
| **exposure** (n) | gradually facing feared situations | экспозиция | Exposure therapy helped him overcome his social anxiety. | exposure therapy, gradual exposure | formal |

### Category 3: ACT & Psychodynamic

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **ACT** | Acceptance and Commitment Therapy — accepting thoughts while committing to values | ТПО (терапия принятия и ответственности) | ACT teaches that thoughts are not facts. | ACT therapy, ACT approach | formal |
| **acceptance** (n) | willingness to experience thoughts and feelings without fighting them | принятие | Acceptance doesn't mean giving up — it means stopping the struggle. | radical acceptance, practice acceptance | neutral |
| **values** (n pl) | what matters most to you; guiding principles | ценности | ACT helps people live in alignment with their values. | core values, live according to values | neutral |
| **defusion** (n) | separating yourself from your thoughts (seeing them as just thoughts) | дефьюжн (разделение с мыслями) | Cognitive defusion means observing thoughts without believing them. | cognitive defusion, practice defusion | formal |
| **psychodynamic** (adj) | relating to therapy that explores unconscious processes | психодинамический | Psychodynamic therapy explores how the past influences the present. | psychodynamic therapy/approach | formal |
| **unconscious** (adj/n) | the part of the mind not in conscious awareness | бессознательное | Psychodynamic theory suggests that many behaviors are driven by unconscious processes. | the unconscious, unconscious mind/processes | neutral |
| **transference** (n) | projecting feelings about someone onto the therapist | перенос | Transference can reveal important patterns in relationships. | positive/negative transference | formal |
| **insight** (n) | deep understanding of yourself or a situation | инсайт, озарение | Therapy gave her the insight to understand why she feared intimacy. | gain/develop insight, a moment of insight | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **work on** | to try to improve something | работать над | I'm working on my anxiety in therapy. |
| **open up** | to start sharing feelings | открыться | It took months before he could open up to his therapist. |
| **break through** | to overcome a barrier | преодолеть барьер | She finally broke through years of denial. |
| **come to terms with** | to accept something difficult | смириться с, принять | Therapy helped him come to terms with his loss. |
| **act on** | to take action based on something | действовать на основе | ACT teaches us not to act on every thought we have. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **get to the root of** | to find the underlying cause | докопаться до сути | Psychodynamic therapy aims to get to the root of the problem. |
| **a turning point** | a moment when everything changes | переломный момент | That therapy session was a turning point in my recovery. |
| **face your demons** | to confront fears or problems you've been avoiding | встретиться со своими демонами | Therapy is about facing your demons, not running from them. |
| **come a long way** | to have made significant progress | далеко продвинуться | She's come a long way since her first session. |

### Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks

Maria wished she (1)_______ started therapy sooner. When she finally found a therapist, building (2)_______ took several sessions. The therapist used a (3)_______ approach, helping Maria explore how her childhood experiences influenced her present behavior.

During one session, Maria had a major (4)_______: she realized that her fear of abandonment stemmed from (5)_______ patterns she'd never been aware of. This (6)_______ gave her the understanding she needed to begin changing.

Later, her therapist introduced some (7)_______ techniques, including thought (8)_______ to track her negative (9)_______ thoughts. She also learned about cognitive (10)_______ — the idea that her thoughts were just thoughts, not facts she needed to (11)_______ on.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. had  2. rapport  3. psychodynamic  4. breakthrough  5. unconscious  6. insight  7. CBT  8. records  9. automatic  10. defusion  11. act
</details>

### Exercise 3: CBT vs ACT vs Psychodynamic

Which approach does each statement describe?

1. "Let's explore how your relationship with your father affects your current partnerships." → _______
2. "Let's test whether your belief 'Everyone judges me' is actually true." → _______
3. "Notice the thought. Label it. Let it pass without acting on it." → _______
4. "What are the values that matter most to you? Are you living in line with them?" → _______
5. "Your unconscious mind may be repeating patterns you learned in childhood." → _______

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. Psychodynamic  2. CBT  3. ACT  4. ACT  5. Psychodynamic
</details>
