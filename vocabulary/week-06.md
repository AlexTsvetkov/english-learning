## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Trauma

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **trauma** (n) | a deeply distressing experience; the lasting impact | травма (психологическая) | Childhood trauma can shape personality for decades. | childhood/emotional/psychological trauma, experience trauma | neutral |
| **PTSD** (n) | Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder | ПТСР | PTSD can develop weeks or months after the event. | suffer from/develop/diagnose PTSD | neutral |
| **flashback** (n) | a vivid, involuntary memory of a traumatic event | флешбэк | She has flashbacks of the accident that feel completely real. | have/experience/trigger a flashback, emotional flashback | neutral |
| **dissociation** (n) | feeling disconnected from yourself or reality | диссоциация | Dissociation is the mind's way of protecting itself from pain. | experience dissociation, dissociative episode | formal |
| **hypervigilance** (n) | state of being constantly on alert for danger | сверхбдительность | Hypervigilance left him unable to relax in public. | experience hypervigilance, a state of hypervigilance | formal |
| **wound** (n) | emotional injury (metaphorical) | рана (эмоциональная) | Some emotional wounds take years to heal. | emotional/psychological wound, deep wound, heal a wound | neutral |
| **survivor** (n) | someone who has lived through trauma | выживший, переживший | She identifies as a trauma survivor, not a victim. | trauma/abuse survivor, survivor of | neutral |

### Category 2: Healing & Recovery

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **resilience** (n) | ability to recover from adversity | устойчивость, жизнестойкость | Resilience isn't about not feeling pain — it's about recovering. | build/develop/show resilience, emotional resilience | neutral |
| **recovery** (n) | the process of returning to health | восстановление, выздоровление | Recovery from trauma is not linear — there are ups and downs. | recovery process, road to recovery, full recovery | neutral |
| **heal** (v) | to become healthy again (physically or emotionally) | исцелиться, залечить | It is believed that the body can heal when the mind feels safe. | heal from trauma, heal emotional wounds, healing process | neutral |
| **post-traumatic growth** | positive personal change after struggling with trauma | посттравматический рост | Post-traumatic growth shows that pain can lead to transformation. | experience post-traumatic growth | formal |
| **EMDR** (n) | Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (therapy) | ДПДГ | EMDR is said to be effective for processing traumatic memories. | EMDR therapy/session/treatment | formal |
| **safe space** (n) | an environment where one feels secure enough to be vulnerable | безопасное пространство | Therapy provides a safe space to explore painful memories. | create/provide a safe space | neutral |
| **processing** (n) | working through emotions and experiences | проработка, переработка | Processing grief takes time and often professional support. | emotional processing, process trauma/emotions | neutral |

### Category 3: Emotional Responses to Trauma

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **shame** (n) | painful feeling of being flawed or unworthy | стыд | Shame is one of the most destructive emotions trauma produces. | feel/carry/internalize shame, toxic shame | neutral |
| **guilt** (n) | feeling responsible for something bad | вина | Survivors often carry guilt, even when the trauma wasn't their fault. | feel/carry guilt, survivor's guilt | neutral |
| **grief** (n) | deep sadness, especially after loss | горе, скорбь | Grief after trauma is not just about death — it can be for lost innocence. | experience/process grief, complicated grief | neutral |
| **numbness** (n) | inability to feel emotions | онемение (эмоциональное) | Emotional numbness is a common defense mechanism. | emotional numbness, a sense of numbness | neutral |
| **helplessness** (n) | feeling of being unable to help yourself | беспомощность | Learned helplessness occurs when people believe nothing they do matters. | learned helplessness, feeling of helplessness | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **get over** | to recover from something difficult | пережить, преодолеть | You don't just "get over" trauma — you learn to live with it. |
| **go through** | to experience something difficult | пройти через | Nobody knows what she went through during those years. |
| **work through** | to deal with problems by thinking/talking about them | проработать | Therapy helped him work through his childhood trauma. |
| **break free** | to escape from something that holds you back | вырваться, освободиться | She finally broke free from the cycle of abuse. |
| **come through** | to survive a difficult experience | пережить, выстоять | Despite everything, she came through stronger. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **what doesn't kill you makes you stronger** | adversity builds strength | что нас не убивает, делает сильнее | "What doesn't kill you makes you stronger" — though it's more complicated than that. |
| **open old wounds** | to bring up painful past experiences | бередить старые раны | Talking about her childhood opened old wounds she thought had healed. |
| **the tip of the iceberg** | only a small visible part of a larger problem | верхушка айсберга | The panic attacks were just the tip of the iceberg — the real issue was unprocessed trauma. |
| **light at the end of the tunnel** | hope that a difficult time is ending | свет в конце тоннеля | After months of therapy, she finally saw a light at the end of the tunnel. |
