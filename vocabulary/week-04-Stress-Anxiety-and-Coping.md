# Week 4 — Vocabulary: Stress, Anxiety & Coping Mechanisms

## Theme: Stress, Anxiety, and Coping Mechanisms

> *"It's not stress that kills us, it is our reaction to it."* — Hans Selye

## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Stress & Anxiety

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **chronic stress** | long-term stress continuing over extended periods | хронический стресс | Chronic stress can lead to serious health problems. | suffer from, reduce, manage chronic stress | neutral |
| **burnout** (n) | emotional exhaustion from prolonged stress | выгорание | After months of overwork, she experienced burnout. | suffer from burnout, emotional/professional burnout | neutral |
| **panic attack** (n) | sudden episode of intense fear with physical symptoms | паническая атака | His first panic attack happened during a meeting. | have/experience a panic attack | neutral |
| **hyperventilate** (v) | breathe too quickly due to anxiety | гипервентилировать | She began to hyperventilate and had to step outside. | start to hyperventilate | neutral |
| **trigger** (n/v) | something that causes an emotional response | триггер / спровоцировать | Crowded spaces trigger his anxiety. | trigger anxiety/memories, emotional trigger | neutral |
| **restless** (adj) | unable to relax, needing to move | беспокойный | Anxiety made her restless — she couldn't sit still. | feel restless, restless sleep/mind | neutral |
| **on edge** (phrase) | tense, nervous, easily startled | на нервах | I've been on edge all week because of the deadline. | feel on edge, put someone on edge | informal |
| **dread** (n/v) | intense fear or anxiety about something coming | страх, ужас / бояться | She dreaded Monday mornings at that job. | dread doing sth, a sense of dread, fill with dread | neutral |

### Category 2: Symptoms & Reactions

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **insomnia** (n) | inability to sleep | бессонница | Stress-related insomnia kept her awake for hours. | suffer from/chronic insomnia | neutral |
| **fatigue** (n) | extreme tiredness | усталость, утомление | Mental fatigue is as debilitating as physical fatigue. | chronic/mental fatigue, suffer from fatigue | neutral/formal |
| **irritability** (n) | tendency to become annoyed easily | раздражительность | Increased irritability is a common sign of anxiety. | increased/constant irritability | formal |
| **procrastinate** (v) | to delay doing things, often from anxiety | прокрастинировать | When overwhelmed, he tends to procrastinate. | tend to procrastinate, procrastinate on sth | neutral |
| **shut down** (v) | to stop functioning emotionally | закрыться, отключиться | When the stress became too much, she would shut down. | emotionally shut down | informal |
| **tense** (adj) | tight, stiff, anxious | напряжённый | His shoulders were tense from hours of worry. | feel tense, tense muscles/atmosphere | neutral |

### Category 3: Coping Strategies

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **coping mechanism** | a strategy to manage stress | механизм совладания | Exercise is a healthy coping mechanism. | healthy/unhealthy coping mechanism | neutral |
| **mindfulness** (n) | paying attention to the present without judgment | осознанность | Practicing mindfulness reduces anxiety symptoms. | practice mindfulness, mindfulness meditation | neutral |
| **grounding** (n) | techniques to reconnect with the present during anxiety | заземление, техники заземления | Grounding techniques helped her during panic attacks. | grounding techniques/exercises | neutral |
| **resilient** (adj) | able to recover from difficulties | жизнестойкий | Resilient people bounce back faster from stress. | emotionally resilient, become more resilient | neutral |
| **self-care** (n) | activities to maintain your health and well-being | забота о себе | Self-care is not selfish — it's necessary. | practice self-care, self-care routine | neutral |
| **decompress** (v) | to relax after stress | расслабиться, сбросить напряжение | After a hard day, she needs time to decompress. | need to decompress, decompress after work | informal |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **freak out** | to panic or become very anxious | запаниковать | She freaked out when she saw the deadline was tomorrow. |
| **calm down** | to become less agitated | успокоиться | It took him thirty minutes to calm down after the argument. |
| **wind down** | to relax gradually after stress | расслабиться постепенно | I need an hour to wind down before I can sleep. |
| **burn out** | to become exhausted from overwork | выгореть | If you don't take breaks, you'll burn out eventually. |
| **lash out** | to attack verbally when stressed or upset | срываться на ком-то | He lashed out at his colleague, then immediately regretted it. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **at the end of one's rope** | completely exhausted, unable to cope anymore | на пределе | After months of insomnia, she was at the end of her rope. |
| **a nervous wreck** | extremely anxious and unable to function | комок нервов | Before exams, I'm a complete nervous wreck. |
| **take a toll on** | to cause damage or harm over time | нанести урон, сказаться | Years of chronic stress took a toll on her health. |
| **the straw that broke the camel's back** | the final small thing that causes a breakdown | последняя капля | Missing the bus was the straw that broke the camel's back — she broke down crying. |

### Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks

For weeks, Anna had been feeling (1)_______ — she couldn't sleep, couldn't relax, and couldn't concentrate. She was suffering from (2)_______ — lying awake until 3 AM most nights. The smallest things would (3)_______ her anxiety: a phone notification, an email from her boss, even the sound of traffic.

She knew she was heading for (4)_______ — she was at the end of her (5)_______. Her friend suggested trying (6)_______ — sitting quietly and focusing on the present moment. At first, Anna felt too (7)_______ to sit still. But after a few weeks of practice, she noticed the (8)_______ in her shoulders beginning to ease. She also started a (9)_______ routine: walking, reading, and turning off her phone in the evenings. Slowly, she became more (10)_______ — better able to handle stress without falling apart.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. on edge  2. insomnia  3. trigger  4. burnout  5. rope  6. mindfulness  7. restless  8. tension/tense (accept either)  9. self-care  10. resilient
</details>
