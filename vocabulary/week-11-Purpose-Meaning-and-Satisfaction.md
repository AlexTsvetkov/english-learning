# Week 11 — Vocabulary: Purpose, Meaning & Life Satisfaction

## Theme: Purpose, Meaning, and Life Satisfaction

> *"The meaning of life is to find your gift. The purpose of life is to give it away."* — Pablo Picasso

## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Purpose & Meaning

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **purpose** (n) | a sense of meaning and direction in life | цель, предназначение | Having a sense of purpose is linked to longer lifespan. | sense of purpose, find/discover purpose, life purpose | neutral |
| **fulfillment** (n) | deep satisfaction from meaningful activity | удовлетворение, самореализация | True fulfillment comes not from achievement but from contribution. | personal fulfillment, sense of fulfillment, find fulfillment | neutral |
| **meaning-making** (n) | the process of creating meaning from experiences | осмысление, создание смысла | Meaning-making is how we transform suffering into wisdom. | engage in meaning-making, the process of meaning-making | formal |
| **vocation** (n) | a calling; work that feels deeply meaningful | призвание | She didn't just have a job — she had a vocation. | find one's vocation, a sense of vocation | neutral/formal |
| **calling** (n) | a strong inner urge to pursue a particular path | призвание, зов | He felt a calling to help people with mental health struggles. | find/follow one's calling, a true calling | neutral |
| **existential** (adj) | relating to existence and the meaning of life | экзистенциальный | An existential crisis forces you to confront fundamental questions. | existential crisis/question/anxiety/dread | formal |
| **transcendence** (n) | going beyond ordinary limits; spiritual elevation | трансценденция, выход за пределы | Maslow described transcendence as the highest human need. | self-transcendence, moments of transcendence | formal |

### Category 2: Satisfaction & Well-Being

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **flourish** (v) | to grow, develop, and thrive | расцветать, процветать | People flourish when they feel connected and purposeful. | flourish in, help someone flourish | neutral |
| **thrive** (v) | to prosper; to grow strong and healthy | процветать | She didn't just survive her crisis — she thrived after it. | thrive in, thrive on challenge | neutral |
| **well-being** (n) | the state of being healthy, happy, and comfortable | благополучие | Psychological well-being depends on more than material comfort. | mental/emotional/psychological well-being, sense of well-being | neutral |
| **contentment** (n) | a state of peaceful satisfaction | удовлетворённость, умиротворение | Contentment is not the same as complacency — it's peace with the present. | find/feel contentment, deep contentment | neutral |
| **gratitude** (n) | the quality of being thankful | благодарность | Practicing gratitude has been shown to increase life satisfaction. | express/feel/practice gratitude, sense of gratitude | neutral |

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|

| **wisdom** (n) | deep understanding gained through experience | мудрость | Wisdom often comes from suffering, not from success. | gain/acquire wisdom, conventional wisdom | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **live for** | to consider something the most important part of life | жить ради | She lives for her work with disadvantaged children. |
| **look back on** | to think about the past | оглядываться на | Looking back on my life, I realize the difficult times taught me the most. |
| **settle for** | to accept less than you wanted | довольствоваться | Don't settle for a life that doesn't feel meaningful. |
| **aspire to** | to want to achieve something | стремиться к | She aspires to make a difference in mental health policy. |
| **boil down to** | to be essentially about | сводиться к | In the end, happiness boils down to connection and purpose. |
