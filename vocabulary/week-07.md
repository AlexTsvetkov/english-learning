## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Types of Empathy

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **empathy** (n) | the ability to understand and share another's feelings | эмпатия, сопереживание | Empathy is the foundation of meaningful human connection. | show/feel/develop empathy, lack of empathy | neutral |
| **cognitive empathy** | understanding someone's perspective intellectually | когнитивная эмпатия | Cognitive empathy means understanding WHY someone feels a certain way. | develop cognitive empathy | formal |
| **emotional empathy** | actually feeling what another person feels | эмоциональная эмпатия | Emotional empathy can be overwhelming for highly sensitive people. | experience emotional empathy | formal |
| **compassion** (n) | empathy + desire to help alleviate suffering | сострадание | Compassion goes beyond understanding — it drives you to act. | show/feel compassion, compassion for | neutral |
| **sympathy** (n) | feeling sorry for someone (without necessarily sharing their feeling) | сочувствие | Sympathy says "I'm sorry you're in pain." Empathy says "I feel your pain." | express/show sympathy, sympathy for | neutral |
| **pity** (n) | feeling sorry for someone from a position of superiority | жалость | Nobody wants pity — they want understanding. | feel pity, self-pity, take pity on | neutral |

### Category 2: Emotional Intelligence Skills

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **emotional intelligence (EQ)** | ability to recognize, understand, and manage emotions | эмоциональный интеллект | High EQ is a better predictor of success than IQ. | high/low EQ, develop emotional intelligence | neutral |
| **self-awareness** (n) | understanding your own emotions and patterns | самоосознание | Self-awareness is the first component of emotional intelligence. | develop/increase self-awareness, lack self-awareness | neutral |
| **self-regulation** (n) | ability to manage your emotional responses | саморегуляция | Self-regulation means responding thoughtfully rather than reacting impulsively. | practice self-regulation, emotional self-regulation | formal |
| **attunement** (n) | being in harmony with another's emotional state | эмоциональная настройка | A good therapist maintains emotional attunement with their client. | emotional attunement, attuned to | formal |
| **active listening** (n) | fully concentrating on what someone says, showing you understand | активное слушание | Active listening means hearing not just words, but the emotion behind them. | practice active listening, active listening skills | neutral |
| **perspective-taking** (n) | ability to see a situation from another's viewpoint | принятие перспективы другого | Perspective-taking reduces conflict and increases understanding. | practice perspective-taking | formal |

### Category 3: Interpersonal Dynamics

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **rapport** (n) | a close, harmonious relationship based on understanding | раппорт, взаимопонимание | Building rapport with a client is essential in therapy. | build/establish rapport, good rapport | neutral |
| **compassion fatigue** (n) | emotional exhaustion from caring too much for others | усталость от сострадания | Therapists and nurses are vulnerable to compassion fatigue. | suffer from/experience compassion fatigue | formal |
| **emotional labor** (n) | the effort of managing your emotions for others' benefit | эмоциональный труд | Customer service requires enormous emotional labor. | perform emotional labor | formal |
| **non-judgmental** (adj) | not forming negative opinions | безоценочный | A non-judgmental attitude is crucial for building trust. | non-judgmental approach/attitude/listening | neutral |
| **unconditional positive regard** | accepting someone completely without conditions | безусловное позитивное принятие | Carl Rogers believed unconditional positive regard was key to therapy. | show unconditional positive regard | formal |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **reach out to** | to contact someone to offer help or connect | протянуть руку, обратиться | She reached out to a friend when she noticed he was struggling. |
| **tune in to** | to become sensitive to someone's feelings | настроиться на | Good leaders tune in to their team's emotional needs. |
| **look out for** | to take care of, watch over | присматривать, заботиться | In healthy communities, people look out for each other. |
| **pick up on** | to notice something subtle | уловить, заметить | She quickly picked up on his discomfort and changed the subject. |
| **relate to** | to understand someone's experience because of your own | понимать, находить отклик | I can really relate to what you're going through. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **put yourself in someone's shoes** | to imagine being in someone else's situation | поставить себя на чьё-то место | Try to put yourself in her shoes before judging. |
| **have a heart of gold** | to be very kind and compassionate | иметь золотое сердце | She has a heart of gold — always helping others. |
| **shoulder to cry on** | someone who offers emotional support | жилетка, плечо, на которое можно поплакать | Everyone needs a shoulder to cry on sometimes. |
| **feel for someone** | to have sympathy or empathy | сочувствовать кому-то | I really feel for her — losing a job is devastating. |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Empathy vs Sympathy vs Compassion vs Pity

Choose the best word for each situation:

1. "I've been there — I know exactly how painful that feels." → _______
2. "I'm sorry you're going through this." → _______
3. "That's so sad. I feel bad for you." (from a distance) → _______
4. "I feel your pain, and I want to help you through it." → _______

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. empathy  2. sympathy  3. pity  4. compassion
</details>

### Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks
