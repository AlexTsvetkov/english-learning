## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Relationship Dynamics

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **codependent** (adj) | excessively reliant on another person emotionally | созависимый | Their relationship became codependent — neither could function alone. | codependent relationship, codependent behavior | neutral |
| **toxic** (adj) | harmful, damaging to well-being | токсичный | She realized the friendship was toxic and decided to distance herself. | toxic relationship/person/behavior/environment | neutral/informal |
| **dysfunctional** (adj) | not operating normally; unhealthy patterns | дисфункциональный | He grew up in a dysfunctional family where emotions were never discussed. | dysfunctional family/relationship/pattern | neutral |
| **reciprocal** (adj) | mutual, given and received in equal measure | взаимный | A healthy relationship is reciprocal — both people give and receive. | reciprocal respect/trust/effort | formal |
| **intimacy** (n) | emotional closeness and deep connection | близость (эмоциональная) | True intimacy requires vulnerability and trust. | emotional intimacy, fear of intimacy, build intimacy | neutral |
| **enmeshment** (n) | unhealthy closeness where individual identities blur | слияние (нездоровое) | In enmeshment, it's hard to tell where one person's feelings end and another's begin. | emotional enmeshment, family enmeshment | formal/clinical |
| **neglect** (n/v) | failure to give proper care or attention | пренебрежение, запущенность | Emotional neglect in childhood can be as damaging as active abuse. | emotional neglect, child neglect, neglect someone's needs | neutral |
| **manipulation** (n) | controlling someone for your own advantage | манипуляция | She slowly recognized the manipulation in her partner's behavior. | emotional manipulation, manipulation tactics | neutral |

### Category 2: Attachment Styles

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **secure attachment** | healthy bond; feeling safe in relationships | надёжная привязанность | People with secure attachment feel comfortable with closeness and independence. | develop secure attachment, secure attachment style | formal |
| **anxious attachment** | fear of abandonment; constant need for reassurance | тревожная привязанность | Anxious attachment often manifests as clinginess and jealousy. | anxious attachment style, develop anxious attachment | formal |
| **avoidant attachment** | discomfort with emotional closeness; valuing independence | избегающая привязанность | He showed signs of avoidant attachment — he would withdraw whenever things got serious. | avoidant attachment style, dismissive-avoidant | formal |
| **clingy** (adj) | overly attached, needing constant attention | навязчивый, прилипчивый | Her anxious attachment made her feel clingy in romantic relationships. | clingy behavior, feel clingy, come across as clingy | informal |
| **withdraw** (v) | to pull away emotionally; to retreat | замкнуться, отстраниться | He tends to withdraw when conversations become too emotional. | withdraw from, emotionally withdraw, withdraw into oneself | neutral |

### Category 3: Boundaries

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **boundary** (n) | a limit that defines what is acceptable | граница (личная) | Setting boundaries is essential for mental health. | set/establish/maintain/respect boundaries, personal boundaries | neutral |
| **assertive** (adj) | confidently expressing your needs without aggression | ассертивный, уверенный | Learning to be assertive changed her relationships. | assertive communication, assertive behavior, become more assertive | neutral |
| **people-pleaser** (n) | someone who prioritizes others' happiness over their own | человекоугодник | As a people-pleaser, she always said yes, even when she wanted to say no. | be a people-pleaser, people-pleasing behavior | informal |
| **enable** (v) | to make unhealthy behavior possible by not confronting it | потворствовать, позволять | By never confronting his addiction, his family enabled his behavior. | enable behavior, enable someone's addiction | neutral |
| **confront** (v) | to face a difficult situation or person directly | столкнуться, поставить вопрос ребром | She finally confronted her mother about the years of emotional neglect. | confront someone about, confront a problem/issue | neutral |
| **overstep** (v) | to go beyond acceptable limits | перейти границу | He overstepped her boundaries by reading her private journal. | overstep boundaries/limits, overstep the line | neutral |
| **accommodate** (v) | to adjust yourself to please others | приспосабливаться, идти навстречу | She always accommodated everyone else's needs while ignoring her own. | accommodate someone's needs/wishes | neutral/formal |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **push away** | to reject someone emotionally | оттолкнуть (эмоционально) | He pushes people away whenever they get too close. |
| **let in** | to allow someone to know your true feelings | допустить к себе | It's scary to let someone in, but intimacy requires it. |
| **put up with** | to tolerate something unpleasant | терпеть, мириться с | She put up with his criticism for years before leaving. |
| **stand up for** | to defend yourself or your rights | постоять за себя | You need to stand up for yourself when someone crosses your boundaries. |
| **grow apart** | to gradually become less close | отдалиться | Over the years, they grew apart and had nothing in common anymore. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **walk on eggshells** | to be very careful not to upset someone | ходить на цыпочках | I was always walking on eggshells around him, afraid of his reaction. |
| **give someone the cold shoulder** | to deliberately ignore someone | демонстративно игнорировать | After the argument, she gave him the cold shoulder for a week. |
| **bend over backwards** | to make a huge effort to please someone | из кожи вон лезть | She bent over backwards to make everyone happy, but nobody noticed. |
| **draw the line** | to set a limit on what you will accept | провести черту | I love helping friends, but I draw the line at being taken advantage of. |

It was terrifying at first — she was afraid people would leave. But she discovered that healthy relationships are (9)_______: when you respect your own needs, others respect them too. She learned to (10)_______ for herself instead of bending over backwards for everyone.

<details>
<summary>✅ Answers</summary>

2. **codependent** / toxic
3. **people-pleaser**
4. **accommodated**
5. **avoidant attachment**
6. **withdraw**
7. **walking** (on eggshells)
8. **set** (boundaries)
9. **reciprocal**
10. **stand up** (for herself)
</details>
