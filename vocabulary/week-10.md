# Week 10 — Vocabulary: Grief, Loss & Acceptance

## Theme: Grief, Loss, and Acceptance

> *"The reality is that you will grieve forever. You will not 'get over' the loss of a loved one; you will learn to live with it."* — Elisabeth Kübler-Ross

---

## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Grief & Mourning

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **grief** (n) | deep sorrow, especially after someone's death | горе, скорбь | Grief is not a problem to be solved but a process to be experienced. | experience/process grief, overwhelming grief, complicated grief | neutral |
| **bereavement** (n) | the state of having lost someone through death | тяжёлая утрата | Bereavement counseling helped her through the first months. | bereavement counseling/support/leave | formal |
| **mourning** (n) | the expression of grief; the period of grieving | траур, оплакивание | Mourning rituals vary across cultures. | a period of mourning, public mourning | neutral |
| **loss** (n) | the state of no longer having something valuable | потеря, утрата | The loss of a parent changes you forever. | experience/suffer a loss, profound loss, sense of loss | neutral |
| **bereaved** (adj) | suffering from the death of a loved one | потерявший близкого | The bereaved family received hundreds of condolence messages. | the bereaved, recently bereaved | formal |
| **inconsolable** (adj) | so sad that nothing can comfort you | безутешный | She was inconsolable for weeks after the funeral. | utterly/completely inconsolable | neutral |
| **anguish** (n) | extreme mental suffering | мука, страдание | The anguish of losing a child is unimaginable. | mental/emotional anguish, cry out in anguish | neutral/formal |

### Category 2: The Five Stages & Emotional Responses

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **denial** (n) | refusing to accept reality | отрицание | Denial is the mind's way of protecting itself from overwhelming pain. | be in denial, a state of denial | neutral |
| **bargaining** (n) | trying to negotiate to reverse the loss | торг | In the bargaining stage, people think "If only I had done things differently..." | the bargaining stage | neutral |
| **acceptance** (n) | coming to terms with reality | принятие | Acceptance doesn't mean being okay — it means acknowledging what is. | reach/find acceptance, gradual acceptance | neutral |
| **closure** (n) | a sense of resolution or completeness after loss | завершение, закрытие гештальта | Some people never find complete closure, and that's normal. | find/seek/achieve closure, sense of closure | neutral |
| **come to terms with** (phrase) | to gradually accept something painful | смириться, принять | It took years for him to come to terms with his mother's death. | come to terms with loss/grief/reality | neutral |
| **let go** (phrase) | to release emotional attachment | отпустить | Letting go doesn't mean forgetting — it means releasing the pain. | learn to let go, let go of grief/anger | neutral |

### Category 3: Healing & Support

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **solace** (n) | comfort in times of distress | утешение | She found solace in writing about her loss. | find/take/seek solace, a source of solace | formal |
| **condolence** (n) | expression of sympathy for someone's loss | соболезнование | He offered his sincere condolences to the family. | offer/express condolences, a message of condolence | formal |
| **commemorate** (v) | to honor the memory of someone | почтить память | They commemorated her life with a celebration of her achievements. | commemorate someone's life/memory | formal |
| **legacy** (n) | what someone leaves behind after death | наследие | Her legacy of kindness lives on in everyone she touched. | leave a legacy, lasting legacy | neutral |
| **resilience** (n) | ability to recover from grief and hardship | жизнестойкость | Resilience in grief doesn't mean not feeling pain — it means surviving it. | emotional resilience, show/build resilience | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **get over** | to recover from something difficult | пережить, оправиться | People say "you'll get over it," but grief doesn't work that way. |
| **move on** | to start a new phase after loss | двигаться дальше | Moving on doesn't mean forgetting the person you lost. |
| **break down** | to lose emotional control; to cry | не выдержать, расплакаться | He broke down at the funeral and couldn't finish his speech. |
| **hold on to** | to keep something/someone in memory or possession | держаться за | She held on to his letters for decades. |
| **pass away** | to die (euphemism) | скончаться, уйти из жизни | His grandmother passed away peacefully in her sleep. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **time heals all wounds** | pain diminishes over time | время лечит | They say time heals all wounds, but some scars remain forever. |
| **gone but not forgotten** | dead but still remembered | ушёл, но не забыт | She is gone but not forgotten — her kindness touched everyone. |
| **a heavy heart** | deep sadness | с тяжёлым сердцем | With a heavy heart, he packed up her belongings. |
| **come to grips with** | to begin to deal with something difficult | взять себя в руки, осознать | She's still trying to come to grips with the diagnosis. |
