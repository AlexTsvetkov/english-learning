## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Cognitive Distortions & Thinking Errors

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **cognitive distortion** | a biased way of thinking that isn't based on reality | когнитивное искажение | Catastrophizing is one of the most common cognitive distortions. | identify/recognize/challenge a cognitive distortion | formal |
| **catastrophize** (v) | to imagine the worst possible outcome | катастрофизировать | She tends to catastrophize — one mistake and she thinks everything is ruined. | tend to catastrophize, stop catastrophizing | neutral |
| **overgeneralize** (v) | to make a broad conclusion from a single event | чрезмерно обобщать | He overgeneralized from one rejection: "Nobody will ever love me." | tend to overgeneralize, overgeneralize from | formal |
| **all-or-nothing thinking** | seeing things in black and white, with no middle ground | чёрно-белое мышление | All-or-nothing thinking makes you feel like a total failure if you're not perfect. | fall into all-or-nothing thinking, exhibit | formal/neutral |
| **mind-reading** | assuming you know what others are thinking | чтение мыслей | She was mind-reading again: "They must think I'm boring." | engage in mind-reading, a form of mind-reading | neutral |
| **ruminate** (v) | to think repeatedly about something distressing | зацикливаться, прокручивать в голове | He spent the whole night ruminating about what he'd said at the party. | ruminate on/about/over, tend to ruminate | neutral/formal |
| **spiral** (v/n) | to quickly get worse; a downward progression of negative thoughts | раскручиваться, спираль | One anxious thought can spiral into full-blown panic. | downward spiral, spiral into, thought spiral | neutral |

### Category 2: Inner Dialogue & Self-Talk

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **inner critic** | the voice in your head that judges and criticizes you | внутренний критик | My inner critic tells me I'm not good enough. | silence/challenge/quiet the inner critic | neutral |
| **self-talk** | the internal dialogue you have with yourself | внутренний диалог, самовнушение | Negative self-talk can damage your self-esteem over time. | negative/positive self-talk, practice positive self-talk | neutral |
| **self-doubt** | lack of confidence in yourself and your abilities | неуверенность в себе | Self-doubt prevented her from applying for the job. | overcome/struggle with self-doubt, plagued by self-doubt | neutral |
| **self-compassion** | being kind to yourself when you suffer or fail | самосострадание | Practicing self-compassion doesn't mean making excuses — it means treating yourself with kindness. | practice/show/develop self-compassion | neutral/formal |
| **harsh** (adj) | severe, cruel, unkind | жёсткий, суровый | She was incredibly harsh on herself after the mistake. | harsh criticism, harsh words, be harsh on yourself | neutral |
| **judgemental** (adj) | forming opinions about people quickly and critically | осуждающий | His inner voice was deeply judgemental. | overly judgemental, judgemental attitude, judgemental of | neutral |
| **irrational** (adj) | not based on logic or reason | иррациональный, нерациональный | She knew the fear was irrational, but she couldn't stop it. | irrational fear/thought/belief, completely irrational | neutral |

### Category 3: Responding to Negative Thoughts

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **challenge** (v) | to question the truth of a thought or belief | оспаривать, подвергать сомнению | The therapist taught me to challenge my negative thoughts. | challenge a thought/belief/assumption | neutral |
| **reframe** (v) | to see something from a different, more helpful perspective | переосмыслить | Instead of "I failed," try reframing it as "I learned something." | reframe a thought/situation/experience | neutral/formal |
| **mindful** (adj) | aware of your thoughts and feelings in the present moment | осознанный | Being mindful of your self-talk is the first step to changing it. | be mindful of, mindful awareness, mindful practice | neutral |
| **internalize** (v) | to absorb beliefs or messages and make them part of yourself | интернализировать, усвоить | Children internalize criticism from their parents. | internalize beliefs/messages/criticism | formal |
| **validate** (v) | to acknowledge someone's feelings as real and important | подтвердить, признать обоснованность | A good therapist validates your emotions without judging them. | validate feelings/emotions/experience, feel validated | neutral |
| **compassionate** (adj) | showing kindness and concern for others' suffering | сочувствующий, сострадательный | Try to be as compassionate with yourself as you would be with a friend. | compassionate response, compassionate toward | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **beat yourself up** | to criticize yourself harshly | ругать себя, корить себя | Stop beating yourself up — everyone makes mistakes. |
| **talk yourself into** | to convince yourself to do something | убедить себя (сделать) | She talked herself into believing she wasn't worthy. |
| **talk yourself out of** | to convince yourself NOT to do something | отговорить себя | I keep talking myself out of asking for help. |
| **snap out of** | to suddenly stop being in a negative mental state | встряхнуться, выйти из состояния | I wish I could just snap out of this negativity. |
| **dwell on** | to think about something too much, especially something negative | зацикливаться на | Try not to dwell on past mistakes. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian Equivalent | Example |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|---------|
| **be your own worst enemy** | to harm yourself through your own behavior/thinking | быть врагом самому себе | When it comes to self-criticism, I'm my own worst enemy. |
| **a vicious circle** | a situation where one problem causes another, making the first worse | порочный круг | Negative self-talk creates a vicious circle: you feel bad, so you criticize yourself, which makes you feel worse. |
| **easier said than done** | something that sounds simple but is difficult in practice | легче сказать, чем сделать | "Just think positively!" — easier said than done when you're in a depressive episode. |
| **give yourself a hard time** | to criticize or blame yourself excessively | быть к себе слишком строгим | Don't give yourself such a hard time — you did your best. |

### Exercise 3: Odd One Out

### Exercise 5: Translation (Russian → English)

1. Мой внутренний критик убеждает меня, что я ни на что не способен.
2. Я склонна катастрофизировать: одна маленькая ошибка — и мне кажется, что всё пропало.
3. Терапевт научил меня оспаривать негативные мысли и переосмыслять ситуации.
4. Хватит себя корить — все совершают ошибки.

<details>
