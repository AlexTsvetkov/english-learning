## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Personal Transformation

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **transformation** (n) | a thorough change in form or character | трансформация | Her transformation from self-doubt to self-acceptance was profound. | personal/profound/radical transformation, undergo a transformation | neutral |
| **metamorphosis** (n) | a complete change (literary/dramatic) | метаморфоза | Therapy triggered a kind of metamorphosis — she became someone new. | undergo a metamorphosis, complete metamorphosis | formal/literary |
| **catalyst** (n) | something that causes or accelerates change | катализатор | The loss of her job was the catalyst for her personal growth journey. | a catalyst for change, act as a catalyst | neutral |
| **turning point** (n) | a moment when everything changes direction | переломный момент | Starting therapy was the turning point in my life. | a major/decisive turning point, reach a turning point | neutral |
| **awakening** (n) | a moment of becoming aware | пробуждение, осознание | Her emotional awakening began when she started naming her feelings. | spiritual/emotional awakening, a rude awakening | neutral |
| **evolution** (n) | gradual development over time | эволюция, развитие | Personal evolution is not a straight line — it's messy and nonlinear. | personal evolution, gradual evolution | neutral |
| **rebirth** (n) | a new beginning after a difficult period | перерождение | After hitting rock bottom, she experienced a kind of rebirth. | experience a rebirth, a sense of rebirth | neutral/literary |

### Category 2: Growth Mindset

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **growth mindset** (n) | belief that abilities can be developed through effort | установка на рост | A growth mindset transforms failures into learning opportunities. | develop/cultivate a growth mindset, have a growth mindset | neutral |
| **fixed mindset** (n) | belief that abilities are innate and unchangeable | фиксированная установка | A fixed mindset says "I can't change." A growth mindset says "I can't change **yet**." | have/be stuck in a fixed mindset | neutral |
| **self-actualization** (n) | realizing one's full potential | самоактуализация | Self-actualization requires authenticity, purpose, and self-awareness. | achieve/pursue self-actualization | formal |
| **agency** (n) | the capacity to act and make choices | агентность, субъектность | Therapy restored her sense of agency — she felt in control again. | sense of agency, personal agency, restore agency | formal |
| **empowerment** (n) | gaining confidence and control over your life | расширение возможностей | Education is a powerful tool for empowerment. | personal empowerment, a sense of empowerment | neutral |
| **self-efficacy** (n) | belief in your ability to succeed | самоэффективность | High self-efficacy is the best predictor of whether someone will persist. | develop/increase self-efficacy, high/low self-efficacy | formal |

### Category 3: Reflection & Integration

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **introspection** (n) | the examination of your own thoughts and feelings | интроспекция | Introspection is the doorway to self-knowledge. | practice/engage in introspection, deep introspection | formal |
| **hindsight** (n) | understanding after the event | ретроспекция, взгляд назад | In hindsight, the crisis was the best thing that happened to me. | in hindsight, with the benefit of hindsight | neutral |
| **narrative** (n) | the story you tell about yourself and your life | нарратив, история | Changing your personal narrative can change how you experience life. | personal/life narrative, rewrite your narrative | neutral |
| **integration** (n) | combining different parts into a coherent whole | интеграция | Emotional integration means accepting all parts of yourself. | emotional/psychological integration | formal |
| **wholeness** (n) | the state of being complete; nothing is missing | целостность | Therapy helped her move from fragmentation to wholeness. | a sense of wholeness, emotional wholeness | neutral/formal |
| **self-compassion** (n) | being kind to yourself in times of suffering | самосострадание | Self-compassion is the thread that runs through every stage of growth. | practice/develop self-compassion, show self-compassion | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **look back on** | to reflect on the past | оглядываться на | Looking back on this journey, I'm proud of how far I've come. |
| **build on** | to use something as a foundation for further progress | строить на основе | Each week's lessons build on the previous ones. |
| **come into your own** | to reach your full potential | раскрыться, найти себя | After years of self-doubt, she finally came into her own. |
| **grow out of** | to develop from; to outgrow | вырасти из | His compassion grew out of his own suffering. |
| **pull through** | to survive or recover from something difficult | выкарабкаться | Despite everything, she pulled through and emerged stronger. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **come full circle** | to return to where you started, but with new understanding | замкнуть круг | My journey has come full circle — I'm back where I started, but I'm a different person. |
| **turn over a new leaf** | to make a fresh start | начать с чистого листа | After her breakdown, she turned over a new leaf. |
| **see the light at the end of the tunnel** | to see hope after a difficult period | увидеть свет в конце тоннеля | After months of therapy, she finally saw the light at the end of the tunnel. |
| **every cloud has a silver lining** | something good comes from every bad situation | нет худа без добра | Losing my job felt devastating, but every cloud has a silver lining — it led me to therapy. |
