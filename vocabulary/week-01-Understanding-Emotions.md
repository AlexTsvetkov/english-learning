## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Basic Emotions (Nuanced)

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **anxious** | worried and nervous about something that may happen | тревожный, обеспокоенный | She felt anxious about starting therapy. | feel anxious, become anxious, anxious about | neutral |
| **nervous** | worried and slightly afraid | нервный, взволнованный | He was nervous before his first session. | get nervous, nervous about, a nervous wreck | neutral |
| **uneasy** | slightly worried or uncomfortable, not quite at peace | неспокойный, не по себе | Something about his reaction made me feel uneasy. | feel uneasy, an uneasy feeling, uneasy silence | neutral |
| **apprehensive** | worried about something that is going to happen | опасающийся | She was apprehensive about revealing her feelings. | feel apprehensive, apprehensive about, deeply apprehensive | slightly formal |
| **overwhelmed** | feeling too much emotion or having too much to deal with | подавленный, ошеломлённый | I felt completely overwhelmed by grief. | feel overwhelmed, emotionally overwhelmed, overwhelmed with/by | neutral |
| **numb** | unable to feel emotions; emotionally shut down | онемевший (эмоционально) | After the shock, she felt completely numb. | go numb, emotionally numb, feel numb | neutral |
| **content** | quietly happy and satisfied | довольный, умиротворённый | He felt content after a long conversation with his friend. | feel content, content with, perfectly content | neutral |
| **fulfilled** | deeply satisfied because you are doing something meaningful | удовлетворённый (глубоко) | She felt fulfilled after helping someone in crisis. | feel fulfilled, personally fulfilled, deeply fulfilled | neutral/formal |
| **resentful** | feeling bitter about something unfair | обиженный, затаивший обиду | He was resentful toward his parents for years. | feel resentful, resentful of/toward, deeply resentful | neutral |
| **nostalgic** | feeling pleasure and sadness when thinking about the past | ностальгирующий | The song made her feel nostalgic for her childhood. | feel nostalgic, nostalgic for/about, a nostalgic feeling | neutral |

### Category 2: Complex Emotional States

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **ambivalent** | having mixed, contradictory feelings | амбивалентный, противоречивый | She felt ambivalent about ending the relationship. | feel ambivalent, ambivalent about, deeply ambivalent | formal |
| **vulnerable** | emotionally exposed; open to being hurt | уязвимый | Being honest about your feelings makes you vulnerable. | feel vulnerable, emotionally vulnerable, vulnerable to | neutral |
| **conflicted** | having contradictory feelings about something | раздираемый противоречиями | He felt conflicted about leaving his hometown. | feel conflicted, conflicted about, deeply conflicted | neutral |
| **detached** | emotionally disconnected; not involved | отстранённый | She seemed detached during the conversation. | emotionally detached, feel detached, become detached | neutral |
| **agitated** | feeling nervous, troubled, or unable to relax | взволнованный, встревоженный | He became agitated when discussing his childhood. | feel agitated, become agitated, visibly agitated | neutral |
| **melancholic** | feeling deep, reflective sadness | меланхоличный | The rainy evening put her in a melancholic mood. | feel melancholic, a melancholic mood, melancholic tone | slightly formal |
| **euphoric** | extremely happy, elated | эйфоричный | He felt euphoric after his breakthrough in therapy. | feel euphoric, euphoric mood, a sense of euphoria | neutral |
| **irritable** | easily annoyed or angered | раздражительный | Lack of sleep made her irritable all day. | feel irritable, become irritable, irritable mood | neutral |

### Category 3: Emotional Processes

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **suppress** (v) | to deliberately hold back or hide emotions | подавлять (эмоции) | He learned to suppress his sadness as a child. | suppress emotions/feelings/anger, suppress the urge | neutral |
| **acknowledge** (v) | to accept or recognize that something exists or is true | признавать, осознавать | The first step is to acknowledge what you're feeling. | acknowledge feelings/emotions, acknowledge the truth | neutral |
| **identify** (v) | to recognize and name | определить, распознать | Can you identify what emotion you're experiencing right now? | identify emotions/feelings/patterns | neutral |
| **express** (v) | to show or communicate feelings | выражать | She found it hard to express her emotions verbally. | express feelings/emotions/gratitude, express yourself | neutral |
| **bottle up** (phrasal v) | to suppress emotions, keep them inside | держать в себе | He bottled up his anger for years before it exploded. | bottle up emotions/feelings/anger | informal |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **open up** | to start talking about your feelings | открыться, довериться | It took months before he could open up to his therapist. |
| **bottle up** | to keep emotions inside, not express them | держать в себе | Don't bottle up your frustration — talk to someone. |
| **break down** | to suddenly start crying; to lose emotional control | расплакаться, не выдержать | She broke down when she talked about her childhood. |
| **come to terms with** | to gradually accept something difficult | смириться с, принять | He's slowly coming to terms with his diagnosis. |
| **hold back** | to stop yourself from expressing an emotion | сдерживать (эмоции) | She held back her tears during the meeting. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian Equivalent | Example |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|---------|
| **wear your heart on your sleeve** | to openly show your emotions | не скрывать своих чувств | She wears her heart on her sleeve — you always know how she feels. |
| **a weight off your shoulders** | relief from worry or stress | камень с души | Telling the truth felt like a weight off my shoulders. |
| **keep a stiff upper lip** | to not show emotion in difficult situations | не показывать эмоций, держаться | British culture traditionally values keeping a stiff upper lip. |
| **hit a nerve** | to mention something that triggers a strong emotional reaction | задеть за живое | His comment about her family really hit a nerve. |

### Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks

Complete the passage with words from this week's vocabulary:

After my first therapy session, I felt strangely (1)_______ — part of me was relieved, and part of me was scared. For years, I had (2)_______ my emotions, pretending everything was fine. I didn't know how to (3)_______ what I was feeling, let alone (4)_______ it to someone else. My therapist said the first step was to (5)_______ my pain instead of running from it.

When she asked me about my childhood, I became (6)_______ — my hands were shaking and I couldn't sit still. But by the end of the session, I felt something I hadn't felt in months: I felt (7)_______. Not happy exactly, but... at peace. It was like (8)_______ — a huge relief.

<details>
<summary>✅ Answers</summary>

1. **ambivalent** (mixed feelings)
2. **suppressed** (held back emotions)
3. **identify** (recognize and name)
4. **express** (communicate)
5. **acknowledge** (accept/recognize)
6. **agitated** (nervous, unable to relax)
7. **content** (quietly satisfied, at peace)
8. **a weight off my shoulders** (relief)
</details>

### Exercise 3: Odd One Out

Which word doesn't belong in each group? Explain why.

1. anxious / nervous / content / apprehensive
2. suppress / express / acknowledge / bottle up
3. euphoric / fulfilled / melancholic / content
4. numb / detached / vulnerable / disconnected
5. resentful / irritable / nostalgic / agitated

<details>
<summary>✅ Answers</summary>

1. **content** — the others all describe worry/anxiety; content is positive
2. **express** — the others mean to hide/keep inside; express means to show
3. **melancholic** — the others are positive emotions; melancholic is sad
4. **vulnerable** — the others describe emotional disconnection; vulnerable means emotionally open
5. **nostalgic** — the others describe negative/tense states; nostalgic is bittersweet
</details>
