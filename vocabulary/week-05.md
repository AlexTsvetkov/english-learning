## Target Words & Phrases

### Category 1: Motivation

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **intrinsic motivation** | doing something because it's personally rewarding | внутренняя мотивация | Intrinsic motivation is more sustainable than rewards. | driven by intrinsic motivation | formal |
| **extrinsic motivation** | doing something for external rewards or to avoid punishment | внешняя мотивация | Bonuses provide extrinsic motivation but don't last. | rely on extrinsic motivation | formal |
| **drive** (n) | inner urge to achieve or succeed | стремление, драйв | She has an incredible drive to succeed. | inner drive, have the drive, lack drive | neutral |
| **willpower** (n) | the ability to control yourself and resist temptation | сила воли | Willpower is like a muscle — it gets tired. | have/lack willpower, sheer willpower | neutral |
| **determination** (n) | firm decision to do something despite difficulty | решимость, целеустремлённость | Her determination to recover was inspiring. | show/have determination, with determination | neutral |
| **discipline** (n) | the ability to make yourself do things consistently | дисциплина, самодисциплина | Success requires discipline, not just talent. | self-discipline, lack discipline | neutral |
| **purpose** (n) | a sense of meaning and direction in life | цель, смысл | Having a sense of purpose increases motivation. | sense of purpose, find/have purpose | neutral |

### Category 2: Habits

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **habit loop** | cue → routine → reward cycle | петля привычки | Understanding the habit loop helps you change behavior. | break/understand the habit loop | neutral |
| **cue** (n) | a trigger that starts a habitual behavior | сигнал, триггер | Stress is the cue that triggers emotional eating. | environmental/behavioral cue | neutral/formal |
| **routine** (n) | a regular pattern of behavior | рутина, привычный порядок | A morning routine sets the tone for the day. | daily/morning routine, establish a routine | neutral |
| **ingrained** (adj) | deeply established, hard to change | укоренившийся | Ingrained habits take months of effort to change. | deeply ingrained, ingrained habit/belief | neutral |
| **consistency** (n) | doing something regularly without interruption | последовательность, постоянство | Consistency is more important than intensity. | with consistency, maintain consistency | neutral |
| **setback** (n) | a problem that delays progress | неудача, откат | One setback doesn't erase all your progress. | experience/suffer a setback, minor/major setback | neutral |
| **relapse** (n/v) | returning to a bad habit after improving | рецидив, срыв | Relapse is a normal part of behavior change, not a failure. | suffer a relapse, relapse into | neutral |

### Category 3: Change & Growth

| Word | Definition | Russian | Example | Collocations | Register |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **accountability** (n) | being responsible for your actions; having someone check on you | ответственность, подотчётность | Having an accountability partner helps maintain habits. | accountability partner, hold someone accountable | neutral |
| **incremental** (adj) | happening in small steps | постепенный | Incremental changes are more sustainable than radical ones. | incremental change/progress/improvement | formal |
| **sustainable** (adj) | able to be maintained over time | устойчивый, долгосрочный | Quick fixes don't work — you need sustainable habits. | sustainable change/habit/approach | neutral |
| **self-sabotage** (n/v) | unconsciously undermining your own success | самосаботаж | Self-sabotage often comes from fear of failure. | engage in self-sabotage, self-sabotaging behavior | neutral |
| **breakthrough** (n) | a sudden important discovery or moment of progress | прорыв | After months of struggle, she had a breakthrough. | make/have a breakthrough, major breakthrough | neutral |
| **momentum** (n) | the force gained by continuing to move forward | инерция, набранный темп | Once you build momentum, it becomes easier. | build/gain/lose momentum, keep the momentum going | neutral |

## Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **give up** | to stop trying | сдаться, бросить | Don't give up — progress isn't always visible. |
| **stick to** | to continue doing something consistently | придерживаться | It's hard to stick to a diet when you're stressed. |
| **fall back into** | to return to old habits | скатиться обратно | After vacation, she fell back into her old patterns. |
| **keep up** | to continue at the same level | поддерживать темп | It's hard to keep up the routine when motivation drops. |
| **cut down on** | to reduce the amount of something | сократить, уменьшить | He's trying to cut down on screen time before bed. |

## Idioms & Fixed Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Russian | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **old habits die hard** | it's very difficult to change established behavior | привычка — вторая натура | I know I should exercise, but old habits die hard. |
| **go cold turkey** | to suddenly stop a bad habit completely | бросить резко, разом | He went cold turkey on social media for a month. |
| **get into the swing of things** | to become comfortable with a new routine | войти в ритм | It took a few weeks to get into the swing of things. |
| **Rome wasn't built in a day** | big changes take time | Москва не сразу строилась | Don't rush — Rome wasn't built in a day. |

### Exercise 3: Odd One Out

1. willpower / discipline / self-sabotage / determination
2. setback / breakthrough / relapse / failure
3. stick to / give up / fall back into / cut down on
4. routine / consistency / relapse / discipline
5. intrinsic / extrinsic / sustainable / incremental

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. **self-sabotage** — negative/harmful; others are positive qualities
2. **breakthrough** — positive progress; others are negative outcomes
3. **stick to** — means to continue; others involve stopping or reducing
4. **relapse** — a failure/setback; others support habit formation
5. **intrinsic** — about motivation source; the others describe qualities of change (though extrinsic is also motivation — accept either intrinsic/extrinsic as odd since sustainable/incremental describe change quality)
</details>
