## Model Text

### *The Hidden Cost of Chronic Stress*

In modern society, stress has become so common that many people accept it as a normal part of life. However, when stress becomes chronic, it can have devastating consequences for both physical and mental health. Understanding the causes and effects of prolonged stress is essential for developing effective coping strategies.

There are numerous factors that contribute to chronic stress. One of the primary causes is work-related pressure: demanding deadlines, long hours, and job insecurity can all lead to burnout. Additionally, relationship problems, financial difficulties, and unresolved emotional issues stem from — and feed into — a cycle of anxiety. As a result of living in a constant state of alertness, the body produces excessive cortisol, which over time takes a toll on the immune system, cardiovascular health, and mental well-being.

The effects of chronic stress are far-reaching. Due to elevated cortisol levels, individuals may suffer from insomnia, chronic fatigue, and irritability. Consequently, their relationships and work performance deteriorate, which in turn creates additional stressors. This leads to a vicious circle: stress causes poor sleep, poor sleep triggers more anxiety, and increased anxiety results in even greater stress. Many people develop unhealthy coping mechanisms — such as overeating, excessive screen time, or procrastination — which provide temporary relief but ultimately make the problem worse.

Fortunately, research shows that healthy coping strategies can break this cycle. Mindfulness, regular exercise, and strong social connections have all been shown to reduce stress levels significantly. The key is to address the root causes of stress rather than merely treating its symptoms. Only by understanding the chain of cause and effect can we begin to build genuine resilience.

## Writing Technique: Cause and Effect Language

### Expressing Causes

| Phrase | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **because of** + noun | Because of chronic stress, she developed insomnia. |
| **due to** + noun | Due to work pressure, burnout rates have increased. |
| **as a result of** + noun/-ing | As a result of ignoring his anxiety, it got worse. |
| **owing to** + noun | Owing to lack of sleep, her concentration suffered. |
| **stems from** | His irritability stems from unresolved childhood trauma. |
| **is caused by** | Insomnia is often caused by chronic anxiety. |
| **contributes to** | Poor diet contributes to higher stress levels. |
| **is triggered by** | Her panic attacks are triggered by crowded spaces. |

### Expressing Effects

| Phrase | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **leads to** | Chronic stress leads to physical health problems. |
| **results in** | Burnout often results in complete emotional shutdown. |
| **causes** | Anxiety causes both mental and physical symptoms. |
| **gives rise to** | Constant pressure gives rise to unhealthy coping mechanisms. |
| **consequently** | She never took breaks. Consequently, she burned out. |
| **as a consequence / as a result** | He avoided his problems. As a result, they got worse. |
| **therefore** | Stress hormones remain elevated; therefore, the body cannot rest. |
| **which in turn** | Poor sleep affects mood, which in turn damages relationships. |

### Expressing Chains of Cause and Effect

Use these to show how one thing leads to another:

> Chronic stress **leads to** poor sleep, which **in turn causes** fatigue. **As a result**, concentration drops, and **this results in** poor work performance. **Consequently**, the person feels more stressed, **creating** a vicious circle.

### 🚫 Common Mistakes

| ❌ | ✅ |
|----|-----|
| Because of she was stressed... | Because **she was stressed**... (clause) / **Because of** stress... (noun) |
| The reason of his anxiety... | The reason **for** his anxiety... |
| It is caused from stress. | It is caused **by** stress. |
| This leads to he feels worse. | This leads to **him feeling** worse. / This means **he feels** worse. |
