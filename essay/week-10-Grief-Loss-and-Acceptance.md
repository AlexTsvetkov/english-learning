## Model Text

### *Why We Need to Talk About Grief*

How many times have you heard someone say "time heals all wounds"? While well-intentioned, this cliché is not only inaccurate but potentially harmful. By implying that grief resolves itself passively, it discourages the bereaved from seeking support. It is time we challenged society's discomfort with death and loss — and learned to hold space for grief rather than rushing to fix it.

Consider this: in many workplaces, bereavement leave is limited to three to five days. The implicit message is devastating — that a person should be "over" the loss of a parent, partner, or child within a business week. Is it any wonder that so many people suffer in silence, suppressing their anguish behind a facade of normalcy? Having been conditioned to view grief as a problem to be solved quickly, many individuals never allow themselves to mourn fully.

The evidence is compelling. Research consistently shows that unprocessed grief leads to depression, anxiety, and physical illness. Conversely, those given adequate time and support to grieve demonstrate far greater resilience in the long term. Surely, if we genuinely care about mental health, we must address how our culture treats loss.

What can be done? First, we need to normalize conversations about death. Schools, workplaces, and families should create environments where people feel safe expressing pain. Furthermore, bereavement support should be treated not as a luxury but as a fundamental aspect of healthcare. Only by confronting our collective avoidance of grief can we build a society that truly supports those in mourning.

*(247 words)*

## Model Text Analysis: Persuasive Techniques

| Technique | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Rhetorical question** | "How many times have you heard...?" | Engages reader |
| **Challenge a belief** | "This cliché is not only inaccurate but harmful" | Shows critical thinking |
| **Concrete detail** | "bereavement leave is limited to 3-5 days" | Makes argument tangible |
| **Emotional appeal** | "loss of a parent, partner, or child" | Creates empathy |
| **Direct address** | "Is it any wonder...?" | Involves reader |
| **Evidence** | "Research consistently shows..." | Builds credibility |
| **Call to action** | "we need to normalize conversations" | Tells reader what to do |
| **Inversion** | "Only by confronting... can we build..." | Emphasizes final point |
| **Participle clause** | "Having been conditioned to view grief..." | Concise C1 structure |

## Writing Technique: Persuasive Writing

### 1. Rhetorical Questions
- *Can we truly call ourselves compassionate if we rush people through grief?*
- *How would you feel if someone told you to "get over" losing someone you loved?*

### 2. Challenging Common Beliefs
- *It is commonly believed that... However, this view fails to account for...*
- *While many people assume that..., the reality is far more complex.*
- *The popular notion that... is not only simplistic but potentially damaging.*

### 3. Emotional Appeal (Pathos)
| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Vivid imagery** | "She sat in the empty house, surrounded by his belongings." |
| **Direct address (you/we)** | "Imagine losing someone and being told to return to normal in five days." |
| **Specific examples** | "A mother grieving her child. A husband who lost his partner of forty years." |

### 4. Logical Appeal (Logos)
- *Research consistently demonstrates that...*
- *The data clearly suggests that...*
- *Studies have repeatedly shown that...*

### 5. Call to Action
- *It is time we...*
- *We must / We need to...*
- *Only by [doing X] can we [achieve Y].*

### 6. Power Structures
| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Not only... but also** | Not only does grief affect mental health, but it also impacts physical well-being. |
| **It is time + past** | It is time we stopped avoiding this conversation. |
| **Surely** | Surely we can do better than five days of bereavement leave. |
| **Inversion** | Only by acknowledging grief can we heal as a society. |
