## Model Text

### *Why Personal Boundaries Are Essential for Healthy Relationships*

It is often said that love means putting others first. While generosity and care are important, I firmly believe that healthy relationships are impossible without clear personal boundaries. Without them, even the most well-intentioned connections can become toxic and codependent.

To begin with, boundaries protect our emotional well-being. When we fail to set limits on how others treat us, we gradually lose our sense of self. For example, a person in an enmeshed relationship might accommodate their partner's every wish while neglecting their own needs. Over time, this leads to resentment and emotional exhaustion. If such a person had learned to be assertive earlier, they would have maintained a healthier sense of identity. Research consistently shows that people with clear boundaries report higher levels of self-esteem and life satisfaction.

Furthermore, boundaries actually strengthen relationships rather than weaken them. Many people — especially people-pleasers — fear that saying "no" will drive others away. However, the opposite is true: relationships built on reciprocal respect tend to last longer and feel more fulfilling. When both partners understand and respect each other's limits, intimacy deepens rather than diminishes. As Brené Brown has argued, "Clear is kind. Unclear is unkind." In my own experience, the relationships that improved the most were those where I finally found the courage to draw the line.

In conclusion, setting boundaries is not a sign of selfishness but an act of self-respect. If more people understood that healthy boundaries lead to healthier connections, we would see fewer dysfunctional relationships and more genuine, lasting intimacy.

*(242 words)*

## Model Text Analysis

### Opinion Language Highlighted

| Phrase | Function |
|--------|----------|
| "I firmly believe that..." | Stating a strong opinion |
| "Research consistently shows that..." | Supporting with evidence |
| "However, the opposite is true..." | Introducing a counterargument |
| "As Brené Brown has argued..." | Citing an authority |
| "In my own experience..." | Personal evidence |
| "If more people understood..." | Hypothetical (Second Conditional) |

### Grammar: Conditionals Used
- "If such a person **had learned** to be assertive earlier, they **would have maintained**..." (Third Conditional)
- "If more people **understood** that..., we **would see**..." (Second Conditional)

## Writing Technique: Expressing and Supporting Opinions

### Stating Your Opinion

| Strength | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| **Strong** | I firmly believe that... / I am convinced that... / There is no doubt that... |
| **Moderate** | In my opinion... / I would argue that... / It seems to me that... |
| **Cautious** | I tend to think that... / It could be argued that... / One might say that... |

### Supporting Your Opinion

Every opinion needs evidence. Use this mix:

| Type | Phrases |
|------|---------|
| **Research/Data** | Studies have shown that... / Research suggests that... / According to recent findings... |
| **Expert opinion** | As [expert] has argued/noted/suggested... / In the words of... |
| **Example** | For example... / A clear illustration of this is... / Consider the case of... |
| **Personal experience** | In my own experience... / I have personally observed that... |
| **Logic/Reasoning** | It follows that... / This suggests that... / The logical conclusion is... |

### Acknowledging Other Viewpoints

Strong essays show you've considered the opposing side:

| Phrase | Example |
|--------|---------|
| While it is true that... | While it is true that some people find boundary-setting selfish... |
| Some people may argue that... | Some may argue that boundaries create distance in relationships. |
| Admittedly... | Admittedly, learning to say "no" can feel uncomfortable at first. |
| Although... | Although open communication is important, it does not mean having no limits. |

Then counter with: *However... / Nevertheless... / On the other hand... / Despite this...*

### 🚫 Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|---------|--------------|
| "I think that boundaries is important." | "I believe that boundaries **are** important." |
| "In my opinion, I think that..." (redundant) | "In my opinion, boundaries are..." OR "I think that boundaries are..." |
| "Everyone knows that..." (too absolute) | "It is widely recognized that..." |
| Starting every sentence with "I think" | Vary: "I believe / In my view / It appears / Evidence suggests" |

## Writing Task

**"Do you agree or disagree: Setting personal boundaries is the most important skill for maintaining healthy relationships. Support your opinion with reasons and examples."**

Write 250–350 words.
