## Writing Technique: The Self-Editing Process

### Step 1: Content Check (The Big Picture)

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Does my essay answer the question/prompt? | ☐ |
| Is my thesis clear and arguable? | ☐ |
| Does each paragraph support my thesis? | ☐ |
| Is there anything irrelevant I should cut? | ☐ |
| Is there a logical flow from paragraph to paragraph? | ☐ |

### Step 2: Structure Check

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Does my introduction have a hook, context, and thesis? | ☐ |
| Does each body paragraph have a topic sentence? | ☐ |
| Does each paragraph have a concluding sentence? | ☐ |
| Does my conclusion restate the thesis and leave an impression? | ☐ |

### Step 3: Grammar Check (C1 Focus)

Go through your essay looking for ONE grammar category at a time:

| Pass | What to look for |
|------|-----------------|
| **Pass 1: Articles** | a/an/the/∅ — is every article correct? |
| **Pass 2: Tenses** | Are tense choices natural? Any unnecessary shifts? |
| **Pass 3: Prepositions** | interested in, depend on, result in, consist of... |
| **Pass 4: Complex structures** | Any participle clauses, inversions, or cleft sentences used correctly? |
| **Pass 5: Word order** | Is word order natural in all clauses? |

### Step 4: Vocabulary & Style Check

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Have I repeated any word more than twice? Can I use a synonym? | ☐ |
| Have I used a range of C1-level vocabulary? | ☐ |
| Is my register consistent (formal throughout, or informal throughout)? | ☐ |
| Have I used hedging where appropriate? | ☐ |
| Have I mixed sentence lengths for variety? | ☐ |

### Step 5: Cohesion Check

| Question | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Have I used linking words between sentences? | ☐ |
| Have I used reference words (this, such, the former)? | ☐ |
| Does each paragraph flow into the next? | ☐ |
| Have I avoided starting every sentence the same way? | ☐ |

### Step 6: Read Aloud

Read your essay out loud. You'll catch:
- Awkward phrasing
- Missing words
- Sentences that are too long
- Unnatural rhythm

## Common B2→C1 Errors: Final Checklist

| Error Type | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Articles | The happiness is important. | **Happiness** is important. |
| Articles | She is therapist. | She is **a** therapist. |
| Prepositions | Depend from circumstances. | Depend **on** circumstances. |
| Word order | I can't understand anymore why. | I can't understand why **anymore**. |
| Reported speech | She suggested me to try. | She suggested **trying** / **that I try**. |
| Modals | She must to rest. | She must **rest**. |
| Conditionals | If I would know... | If I **knew** / **had known**... |
| Passive | It is believed, that... | It is believed **that**... |
| Stative verbs | I am understanding now. | I **understand** now. |
| Inversion | Not only she found... | Not only **did she** find... |
| Participles | Experienced loss, she... | **Having experienced** loss, she... |

## Model: Before and After Editing

### ❌ Before (B2-level, with errors)

*The self-awareness is very important for the personal growth. I think that people should to understand their emotions. When I started to go to therapy, I didn't knew what to expect. My therapist suggested me to try keeping a journal. Not only I discovered new things about myself, but I also started to feeling more confident. If I would start therapy earlier, I might avoided many problems.*

### ✅ After (C1-level, polished)

*Self-awareness is fundamental to personal growth. It could be argued that understanding one's emotions is the first step toward meaningful change. When I started therapy, I had no idea what to expect. My therapist suggested keeping a journal — a practice that proved transformative. Not only did I discover things about myself I had never acknowledged, but I also began to feel more confident in expressing my needs. Had I started therapy earlier, I might have avoided many of the difficulties I faced.*

### What Changed?
- ~~The~~ self-awareness → **Self-awareness** (no article with abstract noun)
- ~~should to~~ → implied by "It could be argued that..." (hedging)
- ~~didn't knew~~ → **had no idea** (more natural + richer vocabulary)
- ~~suggested me to try~~ → **suggested keeping** (correct pattern)
- ~~Not only I~~ → **Not only did I** (correct inversion)
- ~~to feeling~~ → **to feel** (infinitive, not gerund after "begin")
- ~~If I would start~~ → **Had I started** (inverted Third Conditional — C1)
- ~~might avoided~~ → **might have avoided** (modal + have + PP)
