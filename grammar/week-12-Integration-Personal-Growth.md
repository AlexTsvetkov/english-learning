## Part 1: Common C1-Level Errors (Comprehensive Review)

### Articles (The Persistent Russian-Speaker Challenge)

Even at C1, articles remain tricky. Key rules to internalize:

| Rule | Example | Common Mistake |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **The** with unique/specific things | **The** unconscious mind plays a role. | ❌ Unconscious mind plays a role. |
| **No article** with abstract uncountable nouns (general) | **Happiness** is subjective. | ❌ **The** happiness is subjective. |
| **A/An** for first mention; **The** for subsequent | She has **a** therapist. **The** therapist uses CBT. | ❌ She has **the** therapist. (first mention) |
| **The** + superlative | **The** most important thing is self-awareness. | ❌ Most important thing... |
| **No article** + proper nouns / concepts | **Maslow's** hierarchy; **CBT**; **Post-traumatic growth** | ❌ **The** Maslow's hierarchy |

### Prepositions

| Correct | Common Mistake |
|---------|---------------|
| interested **in** | ❌ interested **of/about** |
| depend **on** | ❌ depend **from** |
| consist **of** | ❌ consist **from** |
| result **in** (cause an effect) | ❌ result **to** |
| result **from** (be caused by) | ❌ result **of** |
| cope **with** | cope **with** is correct ✅ |
| succeed **in** + -ing | ❌ succeed **to** |
| apologize **for** + -ing | ❌ apologize **about** |

### Word Order in Complex Sentences

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|----------|
| I can't understand anymore why she left. | I can't understand why she left **anymore**. |
| She explained me the theory. | She explained the theory **to me**. |
| He is since 2020 in therapy. | He has been in therapy **since 2020**. |
| I want that she changes. | I want **her to change**. |

## Part 2: Style Refinement — Writing Like a C1 User

### Sentence Variety

Mix short and long sentences:

❌ *Therapy is important. Self-awareness is important. Emotional intelligence is important.*
✅ *While self-awareness and emotional intelligence are both crucial, it is therapy — perhaps more than anything else — that provides the structured environment needed for real change.*

### Avoid Repetition — Use Synonyms and Paraphrase

| Instead of repeating... | Use... |
|------------------------|--------|
| important | crucial, essential, vital, significant, fundamental |
| problem | issue, challenge, difficulty, obstacle, barrier |
| good | beneficial, effective, valuable, constructive, positive |
| bad | harmful, detrimental, destructive, damaging, adverse |
| show | demonstrate, illustrate, reveal, indicate, suggest |
| think | believe, consider, argue, maintain, contend |
| many people | a significant number of individuals, numerous experts |

### Sophisticated Sentence Structures (C1 Toolkit)

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Cleft sentence** | **It was** only through therapy **that** I understood myself. |
| **Inversion** | **Never before** had I felt so free. |
| **Participle clause** | **Having explored** all twelve themes, I can see how they connect. |
| **Impersonal passive** | **It is widely acknowledged** that emotional literacy is essential. |
| **Fronting** | **Remarkable** was the transformation she underwent. |
| **Ellipsis** | She wanted to change and eventually did. |
| **Hedging** | This would **appear to suggest** that therapy is beneficial. |

## Part 3: Comprehensive Error Correction Exercise

Find and fix ALL errors in each paragraph. Each contains 3–5 mistakes covering grammar from Weeks 1–12.

### Paragraph 1 (Weeks 1-3: Tenses, Reported Speech, Conditionals)

*She has been struggling with anxiety since she was teenager. Her therapist suggested her to try CBT. If she would know about therapy earlier, she might have avoided years of suffering. She admitted that she was suppressing her emotions for long time.*

<details><summary>✅ Corrected Version</summary>

She has been struggling with anxiety since she was **a** teenager. Her therapist suggested **that she try** / suggested **trying** CBT. If she **had known** about therapy earlier, she might have avoided years of suffering. She admitted that she **had been** suppressing her emotions for **a** long time.
</details>

### Paragraph 2 (Weeks 4-6: Modals, Gerunds/Infinitives, Passive)

*She must to be exhausted — she has been working all day. He avoided to discuss his emotions. It is widely believed, that resilience can be developed. He denied that he wasn't feeling resentful.*

<details><summary>✅ Corrected Version</summary>

She must be exhausted — she has been working all day. (**no "to"**) He avoided **discussing** his emotions. It is widely believed **that** resilience can be developed. (**no comma**) He denied **feeling** / denied **that he was** feeling resentful. (**no double negative**)
</details>

### Paragraph 3 (Weeks 7-9: Relative Clauses, Wish, Inversion)

*Daniel Goleman, that wrote Emotional Intelligence, changed our understanding of EQ. I wish I would know about this earlier. Not only she discovered her true self, but she also inspired others. The therapist, that is specialized in CBT, helped her reframe her thoughts.*

<details><summary>✅ Corrected Version</summary>

Daniel Goleman, **who** wrote Emotional Intelligence, changed our understanding of EQ. (**"that" not used in non-defining**) I wish I **had known** about this earlier. (**past regret = wish + past perfect**) Not only **did she discover** her true self, but she also inspired others. (**inversion after "not only"**) The therapist, **who specializes** in CBT, helped her reframe her thoughts. (**"that" → "who" in non-defining; active voice**)
</details>

### Paragraph 4 (Weeks 10-12: Participle Clauses, Discourse Markers, Style)

*Experienced a major loss, she decided to start therapy. She wanted to find meaning but she didn't know how to find meaning. Nevertheless however, she persisted. The happiness is not a destination but a byproduct of living with purpose.*

<details><summary>✅ Corrected Version</summary>

**Having experienced** a major loss, she decided to start therapy. (**perfect participle for completed prior action**) She wanted to find meaning but didn't know **how**. (**ellipsis — don't repeat**) **Nevertheless**, she persisted. (**don't combine two connectors**) **Happiness** is not a destination but a byproduct of living with purpose. (**no "the" with abstract nouns used generally**)
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. At C1, accuracy in articles and prepositions separates good from excellent
2. Sentence variety and synonym use elevate your writing from B2 to C1
3. Use your C1 toolkit: clefts, inversions, participles, hedging, ellipsis
4. Don't overuse fancy structures — naturalness matters most
5. Keep writing, keep reviewing, keep growing
