## Part 1: Passive Voice Review

### Form: be + past participle

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | People **discuss** trauma openly now. | Trauma **is discussed** openly now. |
| Present Continuous | Researchers **are studying** PTSD. | PTSD **is being studied** by researchers. |
| Past Simple | The experience **traumatized** her. | She **was traumatized** by the experience. |
| Past Continuous | They **were treating** her when... | She **was being treated** when... |
| Present Perfect | Scientists **have discovered** new therapies. | New therapies **have been discovered**. |
| Past Perfect | Nobody **had recognized** the signs. | The signs **had not been recognized**. |
| Future (will) | They **will develop** new treatments. | New treatments **will be developed**. |
| Modal | We **can treat** PTSD effectively. | PTSD **can be treated** effectively. |

### When to Use Passive

1. **The action is more important than who did it:**
   - *New trauma treatments **have been developed** in recent years.*

2. **The doer is unknown or obvious:**
   - *She **was abandoned** as a child.* (by her parents — implied)

3. **Formal/academic writing:**
   - *The study **was conducted** over five years.*

4. **To avoid blame or be diplomatic:**
   - *Mistakes **were made**.* (avoids saying who made them)

## Part 2: Impersonal Passive (B2+ Focus)

This is the key B2 structure. It's used in formal/academic English to report beliefs, claims, and knowledge:

### Form: It + passive reporting verb + that-clause

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| It is **believed** that... | It is believed that childhood trauma affects brain development. |
| It is **thought** that... | It is thought that resilience can be learned. |
| It is **known** that... | It is known that PTSD affects millions of people. |
| It is **estimated** that... | It is estimated that 70% of adults have experienced trauma. |
| It is **claimed** that... | It is claimed that EMDR therapy is highly effective. |
| It is **said** that... | It is said that time heals all wounds. |
| It is **reported** that... | It is reported that trauma survivors show remarkable resilience. |
| It is **widely accepted** that... | It is widely accepted that early intervention is crucial. |

### Alternative Structure: Subject + passive + to-infinitive

| Standard | Alternative |
|----------|------------|
| It is believed that **trauma affects** development. | Trauma **is believed to affect** development. |
| It is thought that **she suffered** abuse. | She **is thought to have suffered** abuse. |
| It is known that **he recovered** fully. | He **is known to have recovered** fully. |
| It is said that **therapy helps**. | Therapy **is said to help**. |

### 🚫 Common Russian-Speaker Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct |
|---------|---------|
| ~~It is considered, that trauma is serious.~~ (comma) | It is considered **that** trauma is serious. (no comma) |
| ~~People are thought that they can recover.~~ | It is thought **that** people can recover. / People **are thought to** recover. |
| ~~She has been said to be a survivor.~~ | She **is said** to be a survivor. (present passive for current belief) |
| ~~The research was been completed.~~ | The research **has been** completed. |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Active → Passive

1. Researchers have identified several types of trauma.
2. The therapist treated her with EMDR.
3. People believe that early intervention prevents long-term damage.
4. Scientists are developing new treatments for PTSD.
5. Nobody had warned her about the emotional flashbacks.
6. Experts claim that resilience can be strengthened.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. Several types of trauma **have been identified** (by researchers).
2. She **was treated** with EMDR (by the therapist).
3. It **is believed** that early intervention prevents long-term damage.
4. New treatments for PTSD **are being developed**.
5. She **had not been warned** about the emotional flashbacks.
6. It **is claimed** that resilience can be strengthened. / Resilience **is claimed to** be strengthened.
</details>

### Exercise 2: Impersonal Passive — Rewrite

Rewrite using "It is + past participle + that..." or "Subject + is + past participle + to...":

1. People say that trauma changes the brain.
2. Experts believe that CBT is effective for PTSD.
3. Scientists have estimated that 60% of people experience trauma.
4. Researchers think that she developed symptoms in childhood.
5. Everyone knows that recovery takes time.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. It is said that trauma changes the brain. / Trauma is said to change the brain.
2. It is believed that CBT is effective for PTSD. / CBT is believed to be effective for PTSD.
3. It has been estimated that 60% of people experience trauma.
4. It is thought that she developed symptoms in childhood. / She is thought to have developed symptoms in childhood.
5. It is known that recovery takes time. / Recovery is known to take time.
</details>

### Exercise 3: Error Correction

1. ❌ ~~The survivors were being helped and they have been given support.~~
2. ❌ ~~It is widely believed, that trauma can be healed.~~
3. ❌ ~~She has been said to be one of the most resilient people.~~
4. ❌ ~~New therapies are been developed right now.~~
5. ❌ ~~People are known that they can overcome trauma.~~

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. The survivors **were helped** and **were given** support. (OR: have been helped and have been given)
2. It is widely believed **that** trauma can be healed. (no comma)
3. She **is said** to be one of the most resilient people.
4. New therapies **are being** developed right now.
5. **It is known that** people can overcome trauma. / People **are known to be able to** overcome trauma.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. Passive = **be + past participle** (in any tense)
2. Use passive when the action matters more than the doer
3. **Impersonal passive** ("It is believed that...") is essential for academic/formal writing
4. Alternative: "Subject + is believed/said/thought + to + infinitive"
5. For past: "is thought **to have** + past participle"
