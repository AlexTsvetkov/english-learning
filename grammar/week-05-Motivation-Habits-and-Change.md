## Part 1: Basic Review

### Gerund (-ing) vs Infinitive (to + base verb)

Some verbs are followed by gerunds, some by infinitives, and some by both (with different meanings).

| Gerund only | Infinitive only | Both (same meaning) | Both (different meaning) |
|-------------|----------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| enjoy, avoid, consider, deny, finish, keep, mind, practice, suggest, risk | want, need, hope, decide, expect, learn, manage, offer, plan, promise, refuse, seem | begin, start, continue, like, love, hate, prefer | stop, try, remember, forget, regret, go on, mean |

---

## Part 2: Verbs with Different Meanings (The Important Ones)

### stop

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| stop + **-ing** | quit doing the activity | He **stopped smoking** last year. (He quit.) |
| stop + **to** + verb | pause in order to do something | He **stopped to smoke** on his way home. (He paused for a cigarette.) |

### try

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| try + **-ing** | experiment with something | I **tried meditating** but it didn't help. (I experimented.) |
| try + **to** + verb | make an effort (possibly difficult) | I **tried to meditate** but I couldn't focus. (I attempted but struggled.) |

### remember / forget

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| remember + **-ing** | recall a past action | I **remember feeling** motivated on the first day. (I recall the feeling.) |
| remember + **to** + verb | not forget to do something | **Remember to take** your medication. (Don't forget.) |
| forget + **-ing** | (less common) lose the memory of doing something | I'll never **forget seeing** her reaction. |
| forget + **to** + verb | fail to do something | I **forgot to set** my alarm. (I didn't do it.) |

### regret

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| regret + **-ing** | feel sorry about a past action | I **regret not starting** therapy sooner. |
| regret + **to** + verb | feel sorry about what you're about to say (formal) | I **regret to inform** you that your application was unsuccessful. |

### go on

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| go on + **-ing** | continue the same activity | She **went on talking** about her goals for an hour. |
| go on + **to** + verb | move to a different activity | After discussing habits, he **went on to explain** motivation theory. |

### mean

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| mean + **-ing** | involve, result in | Changing habits **means making** uncomfortable choices. |
| mean + **to** + verb | intend | I didn't **mean to hurt** your feelings. |

## Part 3: Advanced Patterns

### Verb + object + infinitive
- I **want** you **to succeed**.
- She **encouraged** him **to keep trying**.
- The therapist **advised** her **to start** a journal.

### Adjective + infinitive
- It's **difficult to break** old habits.
- She was **determined to change**.
- I'm **willing to try** a new approach.

### Preposition + gerund
- She's **interested in learning** about habit formation.
- He **succeeded in quitting** alcohol.
- I'm **tired of procrastinating**.
- She **apologized for being** late.

### 🚫 Common Russian-Speaker Mistakes

| Mistake | Why | Correct |
|---------|-----|---------|
| ~~I enjoy to run.~~ | "enjoy" always takes -ing | I enjoy **running**. |
| ~~She suggested to try yoga.~~ | "suggest" takes -ing or that-clause | She suggested **trying** yoga. |
| ~~I want that he changes.~~ | Russian uses «чтобы»; English uses object + infinitive | I want **him to change**. |
| ~~I'm interested to learn.~~ | "interested in" + -ing | I'm interested **in learning**. |
| ~~He avoided to talk about it.~~ | "avoid" takes -ing | He avoided **talking** about it. |

### Exercise 1: Gerund or Infinitive?

1. She enjoys _______ (help) others improve their lives.
2. He decided _______ (start) a morning routine.
3. I can't imagine _______ (give up) coffee.
4. They managed _______ (break) their phone addiction.
5. She avoids _______ (think) about her problems.
6. He hopes _______ (become) more disciplined.
7. I keep _______ (forget) to meditate in the morning.
8. She offered _______ (be) my accountability partner.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. helping  2. to start  3. giving up  4. to break  5. thinking  6. to become  7. forgetting  8. to be
</details>

### Exercise 2: Same Verb, Different Meaning

Choose the correct form and explain the meaning:

1. I stopped _______ (eat) sugar. → Meaning: ___
2. I stopped _______ (buy) a snack. → Meaning: ___
3. I tried _______ (wake up) at 6 AM but it was too hard. → Meaning: ___
4. I tried _______ (wake up) at 6 AM and it actually worked. → Meaning: ___
5. I remember _______ (set) goals at New Year. → Meaning: ___
6. Remember _______ (set) your alarm tonight. → Meaning: ___
7. I regret _______ (not take) better care of my health. → Meaning: ___
8. Changing your lifestyle means _______ (make) sacrifices. → Meaning: ___

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. **eating** — I quit eating sugar
2. **to buy** — I paused (my walk) in order to buy a snack
3. **to wake up** — I attempted but it was difficult
4. **waking up** — I experimented with it and it worked
5. **setting** — I recall the past experience of setting goals
6. **to set** — Don't forget to do this
7. **not taking** — I feel sorry about a past action
8. **making** — It involves / results in making sacrifices
</details>

### Exercise 3: Error Correction

1. ❌ ~~She suggested me to try a new routine.~~
2. ❌ ~~I enjoy to read about psychology before bed.~~
3. ❌ ~~He wants that I change my habits.~~
4. ❌ ~~I'm tired to wait for motivation.~~
5. ❌ ~~She succeeded to quit smoking.~~
6. ❌ ~~He avoids to discuss his lack of discipline.~~

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. She suggested **trying** a new routine. / She suggested **that I try**...
2. I enjoy **reading** about psychology before bed.
3. He wants **me to change** my habits.
4. I'm tired **of waiting** for motivation.
5. She succeeded **in quitting** smoking.
6. He avoids **discussing** his lack of discipline.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. Some verbs take only -ing, some only to + verb, some both
2. When both are possible, the meaning often changes (stop, try, remember, regret, mean)
3. After prepositions, always use -ing
4. "Want someone to do" (NOT "want that someone does")
5. "Suggest doing" or "suggest that someone do" (NOT "suggest to do")
