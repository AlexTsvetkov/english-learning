## Part 1: Defining vs Non-Defining Relative Clauses

### Defining (Restrictive) — No commas
Identifies WHICH person/thing we mean. Essential information.

- People **who show empathy** tend to have stronger relationships.
- The emotion **that I couldn't name** turned out to be grief.
- A therapist **whose approach is client-centered** focuses on the patient's experience.

### Non-Defining (Non-Restrictive) — With commas
Adds EXTRA information. Could be removed without changing the meaning.

- Daniel Goleman, **who wrote *Emotional Intelligence***, popularized the concept of EQ.
- Empathy, **which is often confused with sympathy**, involves actually feeling what another person feels.
- My therapist, **whose approach is very gentle**, helped me open up.

### Key Differences

| | Defining | Non-Defining |
|---|---------|-------------|
| Commas | No | Yes |
| "That" | Can replace who/which | ❌ Cannot use "that" |
| Removable | No (changes meaning) | Yes (extra info) |
| Example | The people **who** listen well make the best friends. | My mother, **who** is a good listener, always understood me. |

## Part 2: Relative Pronouns

| Pronoun | Used for | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **who** | people (subject) | People **who** are empathetic... |
| **whom** | people (object — formal) | The person **whom** I confided in... |
| **whose** | possession | A friend **whose** advice I trust... |
| **which** | things | Compassion, **which** requires practice,... |
| **that** | people/things (defining only) | The skill **that** matters most... |
| **where** | places | A space **where** people feel safe... |
| **when** | times | The moment **when** I understood... |
| **why** | reasons | The reason **why** empathy matters... |

---

## Part 3: Reduced Relative Clauses (B2+)

You can shorten relative clauses by removing the pronoun + auxiliary:

### Active → Present Participle (-ing)
- People **who show** empathy → People **showing** empathy...
- A therapist **who practices** active listening → A therapist **practicing** active listening...

### Passive → Past Participle (-ed/irregular)
- Skills **that are learned** in childhood → Skills **learned** in childhood...
- Emotions **which are suppressed** for years → Emotions **suppressed** for years...

### With preposition
- The book **that I told you about** → The book **I told you about** (pronoun dropped)
- The person **whom she confided in** → The person **she confided in** (pronoun dropped)

---

## 🚫 Common Russian-Speaker Mistakes

| Mistake | Why | Correct |
|---------|-----|---------|
| ❌ The person which helped me... | "which" is for things, not people | The person **who** helped me... |
| ❌ My friend, that is very empathetic... | Can't use "that" in non-defining | My friend, **who** is very empathetic... |
| ❌ The reason because I feel this way... | "because" ≠ "why" after "reason" | The reason **why** I feel... / The reason I feel... |
| ❌ People, who practice mindfulness, are calmer. | Defining clause shouldn't have commas (if you mean "those specific people") | People who practice mindfulness are calmer. |

### Exercise 2: Combine the Sentences

Use relative clauses:

1. Active listening is a skill. It requires full attention and empathy.
2. Daniel Goleman wrote a famous book. The book changed how we think about intelligence.
3. She is the therapist. I told you about her.
4. Emotional intelligence can be developed. Many people don't realize this.
5. He has a colleague. The colleague's emotional awareness is remarkable.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>

1. Active listening is a skill **that/which requires** full attention and empathy.
2. Daniel Goleman wrote a famous book **that/which changed** how we think about intelligence.
3. She is the therapist **(whom/who/that) I told you about**.
4. Emotional intelligence can be developed, **which many people don't realize**.
5. He has a colleague **whose emotional awareness** is remarkable.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Defining** = essential info, no commas, "that" OK
2. **Non-defining** = extra info, commas required, NO "that"
3. **Reduced clauses** = more advanced, more concise
4. **who** = people, **which** = things, **whose** = possession
5. Russian speakers: don't use "which" for people, and watch your commas!
