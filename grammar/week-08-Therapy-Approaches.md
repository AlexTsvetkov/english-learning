# Week 8 — Grammar: Wish / If Only & Subjunctive Structures

## Theme: Therapy Approaches — CBT, ACT, Psychodynamic

### 🌅 Warm-Up

> *"Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is our freedom to choose."* — Viktor Frankl

## Part 1: Wish + Past Simple (Present Wishes)

Wishing for something to be different NOW:

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| I wish + Past Simple | I **wish I understood** my emotions better. | I don't understand them well now. |
| If only + Past Simple | **If only I were** more self-aware. | I'm not self-aware enough. |

**Note:** Use **"were"** (not "was") in formal writing:
- I wish I **were** in therapy right now. ✅ (formal)
- I wish I **was** in therapy right now. ✅ (informal)

---

## Part 2: Wish + Past Perfect (Past Regrets)

Wishing something in the PAST had been different:

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| I wish + Past Perfect | I **wish I had started** therapy earlier. | I didn't start early enough. |
| If only + Past Perfect | **If only I had known** about CBT sooner. | I didn't know about it. |

---

## Part 3: Wish + Would (Annoyance / Desire for Change)

Wishing someone else would change their behavior:

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| I wish + sb + would | I **wish he would open up** about his feelings. | He refuses to. I'm frustrated. |
| If only + sb + would | **If only she would try** therapy. | She won't try it. |

**Important:** Don't use "I wish I would..." Use "I wish I could..." instead.
- ❌ I wish I would stop worrying.
- ✅ I wish I **could** stop worrying.

---

## Part 4: Wish + Could (Ability)

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **wish I could** control my anxiety. | I can't control it. |
| **If only** we **could** turn back time. | We can't. |

---

## Part 5: It's Time / Would Rather + Subjunctive

### It's (high) time + Past Simple
Used to say something should happen now (slightly urgent):

- It's time I **faced** my fears. (= I should face them NOW)
- It's high time she **got** professional help.

### Would rather + Past Simple (for others)
Used when you want someone else to do something:

- I'd rather you **didn't** avoid the topic.
- She'd rather he **talked** to a therapist.

### Would rather + base verb (for yourself)
- I'd rather **try** ACT than CBT.
- She'd rather **not discuss** it.

---

## Summary

| Structure | Time | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| wish + Past Simple | Present | I wish I were braver. |
| wish + Past Perfect | Past | I wish I had gone to therapy. |
| wish + would | Frustration about others | I wish he would listen. |
| wish + could | Inability | I wish I could let go. |
| if only + ... | Same as wish (more emotional) | If only I had known... |
| it's time + Past Simple | Urgent present | It's time I took action. |
| would rather + Past Simple | Preference for others | I'd rather you came with me. |

---

## 🚫 Common Russian-Speaker Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct |
|---------|---------|
| ❌ I wish I can change. | I wish I **could** change. |
| ❌ I wish I would go to therapy. | I wish I **could/would** go... (usually "could") |
| ❌ If only I knew it before. (past meaning) | If only I **had known** it before. |
| ❌ It's time to I started therapy. | It's time I **started** therapy. (no "to") |
| ❌ I'd rather you don't come. | I'd rather you **didn't** come. |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Present Wish or Past Regret?

1. I wish I _______ (be) more comfortable talking about my feelings. [now]
2. If only she _______ (discover) ACT therapy sooner. [past]
3. I wish I _______ (can) control my intrusive thoughts. [now]
4. If only we _______ (address) this issue years ago. [past]
5. She wishes she _______ (not be) so afraid of vulnerability. [now]
6. He wishes he _______ (not say) those hurtful things. [past]

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. were / was  2. had discovered  3. could  4. had addressed  5. weren't / wasn't  6. hadn't said
</details>

### Exercise 2: Wish + Would

Complete with appropriate forms:

1. I wish my friend _______ (stop) avoiding his problems.
2. If only she _______ (be) willing to try therapy.
3. I wish people _______ (not judge) those who seek help.
4. If only he _______ (listen) to his therapist's advice.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. would stop  2. were (ability/willingness — not "would")  3. wouldn't judge / didn't judge  4. would listen
</details>

### Exercise 3: It's Time / Would Rather

1. You've been avoiding this for too long. It's time you _______ (face) your feelings.
2. I'd rather you _______ (not bring) up that topic right now.
3. It's high time society _______ (take) mental health seriously.
4. She'd rather _______ (work) with a CBT therapist than a psychodynamic one.

<details><summary>✅ Answers</summary>
1. faced  2. didn't bring  3. took  4. work (base form — for herself)
</details>

### Exercise 4: Translate from Russian

## Key Takeaways

1. **wish + Past Simple** = present wish (things I want to be different NOW)
2. **wish + Past Perfect** = past regret (things I wanted to be different THEN)
3. **wish + would** = frustration about others' behavior
4. **wish + could** = inability
5. **It's time + Past Simple** = something should happen urgently
6. **Would rather + Past Simple** = preference for what others do
