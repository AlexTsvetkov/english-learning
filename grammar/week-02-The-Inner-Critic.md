## Part 1: Reported Speech (B2 Depth)

### Why This Matters for This Theme

When we talk about our inner critic, we are essentially *reporting* what our mind tells us. "My inner voice told me I wasn't good enough." This is reported speech — and it's one of the most useful structures for therapy, psychology, and self-reflection.

### Basic Tense Shift (Review)

When we report what someone said, the tenses usually shift back:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| "I **am** feeling anxious." | She said she **was** feeling anxious. |
| "I **can't** cope with this." | He said he **couldn't** cope with it. |
| "I **have tried** everything." | She said she **had tried** everything. |
| "I **will** change." | He said he **would** change. |
| "I **must** be stronger." | She said she **had to** be stronger. |

### When NOT to Shift Tenses

At B2 level, you should know that tense shift is **optional** when:

1. **The statement is still true:**
   - *She said she **is** afraid of conflict.* ✅ (still true)
   - *She said she **was** afraid of conflict.* ✅ (also correct)

2. **Reporting with present tense verbs:**
   - *He **says** he **feels** overwhelmed.* (no shift needed)

3. **Universal truths:**
   - *The therapist explained that negative self-talk **affects** everyone.* ✅

### Changes Beyond Tenses

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| this → that | "I feel **this** is wrong" → She said she felt **that** was wrong |
| here → there | "I feel safe **here**" → He said he felt safe **there** |
| today → that day | "I'm anxious **today**" → She said she was anxious **that day** |
| tomorrow → the next day | — |
| yesterday → the day before | — |
| now → then / at that point | — |
| ago → before / earlier | "**two years ago**" → **two years before** |

## Part 2: Advanced Reporting Verbs

### Beyond "Said" and "Told"

At B2+ level, you need to use precise reporting verbs that convey HOW something was said:

| Verb | Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|---------|
| **claim** | claim + (that) | state without proof | He claimed that he was fine, but his eyes said otherwise. |
| **insist** | insist + (that) | say firmly | She insisted that she didn't need help. |
| **deny** | deny + -ing | say something isn't true | He denied feeling jealous. |
| **admit** | admit + -ing / + (that) | confess reluctantly | She admitted (that) she had been avoiding her feelings. |
| **suggest** | suggest + -ing / + (that) | propose an idea | The therapist suggested trying a different approach. |
| **warn** | warn + sb + (not) to | alert about danger | My friend warned me not to ignore my anxiety. |
| **encourage** | encourage + sb + to | give confidence/support | The group encouraged him to share his story. |
| **urge** | urge + sb + to | strongly recommend | She urged me to seek professional help. |
| **reassure** | reassure + sb + (that) | make someone feel less worried | He reassured her that it was okay to cry. |
| **confide** | confide + (that) / + in | share a secret | She confided in me that she'd been feeling hopeless. |
| **convince** | convince + sb + (that) / + to | persuade | The therapist convinced him that his thoughts weren't facts. |
| **blame** | blame + sb + for | say it's someone's fault | She blamed herself for the breakdown of the relationship. |
| **complain** | complain + (that) / + about | express dissatisfaction | He complained that nobody understood him. |
| **promise** | promise + to / + (that) | commit to doing something | She promised to be more honest about her feelings. |
| **refuse** | refuse + to | say no | He refused to talk about his childhood. |
| **apologize** | apologize + for + -ing | say sorry | She apologized for lashing out in anger. |

### Patterns Summary

| Pattern | Verbs |
|---------|-------|
| verb + (that) + clause | claim, insist, admit, complain, reassure, confide, promise |
| verb + -ing | deny, admit, suggest, apologize for |
| verb + sb + to + infinitive | encourage, urge, warn, convince, remind |
| verb + sb + for + -ing | blame, criticize, thank |

### 🚫 Common Russian-Speaker Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct |
|---------|---------|
| ~~He suggested me to try meditation.~~ | He suggested **trying** meditation. / He suggested **that I try** meditation. |
| ~~She denied that she was not sad.~~ | She denied **being** sad. / She denied **that she was** sad. |
| ~~He said me that...~~ | He **told** me that... |
| ~~She insisted to go.~~ | She insisted **on going**. |
| ~~He convinced me that to go.~~ | He convinced me **to go**. |

### Exercise 3: Error Correction

Find and fix the mistakes:

1. She suggested me to see a therapist.
2. He denied that he wasn't jealous.
3. The therapist said me that self-criticism is normal.
4. She insisted to come to the session alone.
5. He convinced me that I should to take a break.
6. She blamed herself for that she failed.

<details>
<summary>✅ Answers</summary>

1. She suggested **that I see** a therapist. / She suggested **seeing** a therapist.
2. He denied **that he was** jealous. / He denied **being** jealous.
3. The therapist **told** me that self-criticism is normal.
4. She insisted **on coming** to the session alone.
5. He convinced me that I should take a break. (remove "to")
6. She blamed herself **for failing**. / She blamed herself **for her failure**.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Reported speech** shifts tenses back, but this is optional when the information is still true
2. **Reporting verbs** convey HOW something was said — "claimed" ≠ "admitted" ≠ "insisted"
3. Watch the patterns: some verbs take -ing, some take to + infinitive, some take that-clauses
4. Russian speakers: remember that "suggest" doesn't take "to" + infinitive, and "deny" takes -ing
5. This grammar is perfect for talking about your thoughts, your inner critic, and what people in your life have said to you
